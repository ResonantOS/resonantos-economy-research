# Approved Language Audition

Audience: people being trained to use the research harness

Room outcome: participants can compare contribution mechanisms as falsifiable
bets rather than treating a recorded contribution or attractive mechanism as a
foregone conclusion

Presenter voice: informal, curious, collaborative, and willing to let the room
attack the presenter's idea

Approval status: **approved by the operator on 2026-07-13**

## Opening

### Projected Copy

> The contribution already counts.
>
> What should happen next?

### Spoken Copy

“The DAO already recognizes things like attending calls, studying, mentoring,
and maintaining context as contributions. We’re not here to decide whether they
count. We’re here to explore what should happen to those contributions over
time—and how they might connect to a valuable result that appears later.”

## Tension

### Projected Copy

> How should earlier contributions connect to a later result?

### Spoken Copy

“Imagine I joined twenty governance calls and later introduced a major
improvement to governance. Should all twenty calls count toward that result?
Only the calls about the same subject? Should their relevance decrease over
time? Who decides which contributions helped? We know the contributions
happened. The challenge is designing the best mechanism for using them without
creating automatic entitlement or contribution farming.”

## Reveal

### Projected Copy

> The contribution is known.
>
> The mechanism is the bet.

### Spoken Copy

“Now we can compare different mechanisms. Maybe contributions accumulate. Maybe
only related contributions can be realized. Maybe nothing happens until a
witnessed result creates a reason to review them. Each mechanism is a bet about
what will produce useful recognition without creating harmful behavior. Our job
is to define the criteria, compare the options, and find out which mechanism
survives.”

## Correction Trace

1. The first audition was too formal and used “work” where the operator wanted
   “contribution.”
2. The second audition asked whether a contribution “mattered,” incorrectly
   reopening a premise the DAO had already fixed.
3. The approved audition keeps contribution status fixed and moves the open
   question to mechanism comparison.

This correction trace is authoring evidence. It is never projected to trainees.
