# Bets, Mechanisms, and Prizes — Live Training Workshop

Status: candidate training package
Promotion scope: local-research-only
Audience: people learning to use this research harness
Default format: 90-minute facilitator-led workshop

## Purpose

This package turns the
[bets, mechanisms, and prizes walkthrough](../RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md)
into a live session. The trainer does not lecture through the repository. They
recreate the research interaction with the room:

```text
fixed contribution premise
  -> open mechanism question
  -> competing mechanism candidates
  -> declared meaning of "best"
  -> falsifiable bets
  -> adversarial attacks
  -> independent recognition / community / communication bets
  -> directed, bounded prior art in each relevant subtower
  -> witness designs
  -> bounded mock verdicts and residue
```

The learning result is not agreement with one mechanism. It is the ability to
turn an attractive mechanism into a research prize, a falsifiable bet, a
collapse-test, a mechanism-directed prior-art route, and a witness without
converting research into DAO policy.

## Fixed Premise

For this workshop, the DAO already counts calls, study, mentoring, maintenance,
and similar activities as contributions. Trainees do **not** debate whether those
activities count.

They investigate how prior contribution records should interact with a later
witnessed result. No mechanism is preselected.

## What “Best Mechanism” Means Here

There is no universal best mechanism in this package. A group must declare its
comparison criteria before choosing among candidates. The default criteria are:

- useful recognition of result-linked contribution;
- low false-credit acceptance;
- resistance to contribution farming;
- bounded review burden;
- fair access for people with different amounts of time and visibility;
- low entitlement and guaranteed-return leakage;
- no automatic reward, payout, reputation, governance, or authority effect.

Changing the criteria may change which candidate looks strongest. That is part
of the lesson, not a defect.

## Workshop-Derived Continuation

[Contribution Measurement, Failure, Review, and
Allocation](CONTRIBUTION-MEASUREMENT-FAILURE-AND-ALLOCATION.md) preserves the
anonymized contribution examples and follow-on mechanism questions raised in a
workshop discussion: contribution families, measurement dimensions, Agile
estimation limits, informative failures, reviewer conflicts, bounded
randomness, history, lotteries, newcomer access, and allocation inflation.

It is a candidate research guide, not part of the already-authored 90-minute
core. Its proposed experiments have not run, and it does not modify the prize or
bet registers.

## Authority Boundary

Workshop outputs are disposable or local training evidence unless the research
owner separately accepts them into the prize register, bet register, dispatch,
definitions, fixtures, or inventory. Completing the workshop closes no prize,
validates no mechanism, and grants no reward or authority.
