## Newcomer-reader trial still required

The smallest useful teaching witness is:

1. Give this guide and a blank scratch directory to someone unfamiliar with the
   harness.
2. Give them a different mechanism proposition.
3. Ask them to produce one prize card, at least two independent bet cards, one
   witness card, and one pre-run adjudication template.
4. Ask them to identify which step needs explicit human confirmation and which
   evidence layer cannot validate the mechanism.
5. Record where they need unstated vocabulary, repository knowledge, or help.

Survival condition: the reader produces structurally complete artifacts, does
not infer payout or authority, treats the mechanism itself as a bet, and does
not merge independent evidence.

Falsifier: the reader repeatedly treats a prize as a reward, fixture validation
as behavioral proof, or one bet's evidence as proof of the others despite using
the guide.

## Learning residue

1. Teach the concrete problem before introducing formal vocabulary.
2. Human corrections are part of the method, not noise to remove from the final
   explanation.
3. A mechanism's direct utility, social effect, and communication safety are
   separate causal claims and need separate witnesses.
4. Two languages can improve explanation only when the operational language
   remains the sole authority for records and consequences.
5. A validation companion should name the missing audience witness instead of
   converting source-backed prose into a usability claim.
