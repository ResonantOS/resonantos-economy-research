# Three Open Research Bets About Deferred Contribution

These are three open research bets about a mechanism idea. They are not a
reward program, an adopted DAO policy, or a validated mechanism.

Here, a **bet** is a prediction we can test and reject. Nobody is wagering
money. All three bets are still open, and none of their experiments has run.

## The starting point

For this proposal, the starting premise is that activities such as joining
governance calls, studying, mentoring, documenting decisions, and maintaining
shared systems already count as contributions.

We are not testing whether those contributions matter. We are testing how
records of earlier contributions might help reviewers understand a person's
connection to a valuable result that appears later.

For example, someone might join governance calls for months, study recurring
problems, and later publish a major governance proposal. Would the earlier
contribution records help reviewers understand how the result became possible?

The candidate process is deliberately limited:

```text
earlier contribution record
  → later witnessed result
  → claimed connection between the two
  → review
  → accepted, rejected, or held
```

A later result opens a review. It does not automatically convert earlier
activity into recognition, money, status, or power.

## Bet 1 — Better recognition

### What we are testing

We predict that reviewers will make better result-specific recognition
decisions when they can inspect:

- the witnessed result; and
- a bounded set of earlier contribution records with an explained connection
  to that result.

The comparison is a review of the result alone. The added records should help
reviewers notice genuine enabling contributions that the result-only review
would miss.

A record does not earn recognition by itself. Attendance, time spent, topic
similarity, or the number of records is not enough. The claimed connection can
be accepted, rejected, or held when the evidence is incomplete or conflicted.

### What would prove this bet wrong

This bet fails if the extra records:

- do not improve correct recognition;
- create too many false connections;
- reward attendance or time without a demonstrated result connection;
- encourage people to farm contribution records;
- create expectations of automatic recognition; or
- add more review burden than useful information.

## Bet 2 — Better community outcomes

### What we are testing

If Bet 1 produces a usable recognition candidate, we predict that careful
recognition limited to one result will improve at least one community outcome
chosen before the test, such as:

- voluntary useful follow-through;
- maintenance;
- mentoring;
- contributor retention; or
- trust in the contribution process.

### What would prove this bet wrong

This bet fails if the chosen community outcome does not improve, or if any
improvement is cancelled by:

- status competition;
- performative contribution;
- entitlement;
- exclusion of people with less time or visibility;
- reduced intrinsic motivation;
- contributor attrition; or
- excessive review burden.

Bet 1 must provide a credible recognition candidate before this test can run.
But Bet 1's evidence cannot be reused as proof that the community benefits.
Recognition accuracy and community health are different questions.

## Bet 3 — A clearer explanation without false promises

We want to test whether two languages can coexist:

| Narrative language | Operational meaning | What it must not imply |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **investment** | an earlier contribution is recorded and may later be offered as evidence | no balance, debt, ownership, or guaranteed return |
| **realization** | a later witnessed result makes a claimed connection reviewable | the connection is not automatically true or accepted |
| **cashing** | reviewers accept a recognition decision limited to one result | no money, token, automatic credit, reusable balance, reputation, or governance authority |

### What we are testing

We predict that presenting the narrative and operational languages together
will make the idea easier to understand without making people believe that
contributions accumulate guaranteed financial value.

### What would prove this bet wrong

This bet fails if the two-language explanation:

- does not improve understanding; or
- makes people more likely to expect automatic recognition, payment,
  reputation, status, or governance power.

If this bet fails, we revise or stop using the investment metaphor. That would
not automatically disprove the operational candidate in Bet 1.

## Why these are three separate bets

| Bet | Question |
| --- | --- |
| **1. Recognition** | Do earlier contribution records improve result-specific recognition? |
| **2. Community** | Does that recognition improve a chosen community outcome? |
| **3. Communication** | Can people understand the idea without expecting a guaranteed return? |

A positive result in one bet does not prove either of the others:

- better recognition does not prove a healthier community;
- a healthier community does not prove the recognition was accurate;
- clearer language does not prove the mechanism works; and
- a confusing metaphor does not automatically disprove the operational idea.

## What this proposal does not promise

Recording a contribution does not automatically create:

- stored financial value;
- guaranteed later recognition;
- payment or payout rights;
- a reputation score;
- governance power or voting weight;
- a reusable contribution balance;
- implementation readiness; or
- proof that the mechanism helps the community.

Every recognition decision remains limited to the reviewed connection between
earlier contributions and one later result.

## A concrete example

Ana regularly joins governance calls and studies problems in the DAO's current
process. Months later, she publishes a new governance method that the DAO can
inspect.

The three experiments would ask:

1. Do Ana's earlier records help reviewers recognize genuine enabling
   contributions that they would miss by looking only at the final proposal?
2. If the recognition is accurate, does it improve a chosen later community
   outcome without creating status games, exclusion, or entitlement?
3. Can we explain the process as investment, realization, and cashing without
   making people think Ana accumulated a guaranteed balance or payment claim?

## Current status

All three bets are open. No attribution comparison, community pilot, or
comprehension experiment has run.

Even if all three pass under their declared conditions, the proposal only
becomes eligible for independent owner review. It is not automatically approved,
funded, implemented, or turned into DAO policy.

> In one sentence: we are testing whether earlier contribution records improve
> recognition, whether that recognition helps the community, and whether the
> idea can be explained without creating false expectations.
