# Two-Lane Dialectics

Status: active
Promotion scope: local-research-only

## Purpose

The user proposed three views: socioeconomic behavior, algorithmic reward/randomness, and existing Evidence Exchange / authority work. This file adds a two-lane dialectic so the research does not only elaborate that proposal.

## Dialectic A: Mechanism-First vs Institution-First

| Lane | Starting belief | What it sees well | What it misses |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mechanism-first | Design scoring, random audits, reputation, and treasury rules correctly and the economy will behave. | Incentive compatibility, collusion, exploit surfaces, measurable invariants. | Legitimacy, identity, morale, status, meaning, and institutional drift. |
| Institution-first | Build rituals, governance, review, education, and community identity first; mechanisms should support them. | Long-term trust, shared norms, contributor identity, stewardship. | Attack surfaces, metric gaming, treasury risk, and ambiguous allocation rules. |

Synthesis question: what is the smallest mechanism that strengthens an institution instead of replacing it?

## Dialectic B: Credit Graph vs Commons Stewardship

| Lane | Starting belief | What it sees well | What it misses |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Credit graph | Make contribution visible, reviewed, and traceable so labor compounds. | Receipts, lineage, reviewability, eligibility, maintenance signals. | Intrinsic motivation, solidarity, unmeasured care work, anti-performative contribution. |
| Commons stewardship | Build norms and practices that preserve a shared resource over time. | Anti-extraction, community health, reciprocity, maintenance. | Who did what, review accountability, allocation conflict, reward eligibility. |

Synthesis question: can the credit graph remain a servant of the commons rather than the commons becoming a servant of the graph?

## Dialectic C: Randomness As Defense vs Randomness As Illegibility

| Lane | Starting belief | What it sees well | What it misses |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Randomness as defense | Random audits, lotteries, and stochastic review make gaming harder. | Anti-farming, anti-sybil sampling, exploration, unpredictability. | Fairness perception, accountability, explanation, stable contributor planning. |
| Randomness as illegibility | People need legible paths from contribution to recognition. | Trust, motivation, clarity, predictable effort/reward relation. | Exploitation of fully predictable rules. |

Synthesis question: where should randomness enter: audit selection, reviewer assignment, challenge sampling, or reward allocation?
