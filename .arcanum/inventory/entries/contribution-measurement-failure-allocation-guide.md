## Summary

This synthesis preserves an anonymized workshop corpus of concrete contribution
examples and organizes it into a candidate research guide. It separates
contribution description, planning, result-specific recognition, and
opportunity allocation; proposes non-hierarchical contribution families and
unscalarized planning/result dimensions; bounds what Agile estimation may show;
classifies informative versus non-informative failures; proposes conflict-aware
review and bounded random assignment; limits history to challengeable context;
and turns newcomer access, allocation inflation, and qualified-pool lotteries
into falsifiable candidate experiments.

The guide keeps the workshop premise fixed: the DAO may already count the named
activities as contributions. It does not reopen that decision. It asks how
evidence should describe, connect, review, and allocate around those
contributions without turning attendance, effort, reputation, failure count, or
history into a universal value or authority score.

All external references are recorded as bounded related-evidence candidates
with source kind and local bridge limits. They are not admitted as official
ResonantDAO sources or as proof of a local mechanism.

## Residue

No candidate experiment has run. The intended meaning of `shared inference?`
is unresolved. No universal task comparison, failure-value function, reviewer
assignment mechanism, qualified-pool rule, history rule, lottery, newcomer
reserve, or allocation design is validated. New working labels require their
named ER-D23 or experiment-owner routes before fixture or policy use. The
existing workshop validation pass does not transfer to this post-validation
extension.
