## Summary

Every proposed mechanism routes a directed and bounded question into each
relevant subtower before its witness design is treated as ready. Relevance is
not automatic: a subtower must name the bet, assumption, collapse-test, or
witness decision it can change. Selected lanes require a declared source bound,
negative precedent, supported/unsupported split, local bridge risk or
disanalogy, and next falsifier. Excluded subtowers receive an explicit reason
kept as residue.

Prior art can revise, split, kill, or sharpen a bet. It cannot emit `SURVIVED`,
validate a mechanism, or create policy or authority.

## How It Runs

```text
prize + bet + collapse-test
  -> relevant-subtower gate
  -> one bounded question per selected subtower
  -> negative precedent + source/bridge boundary
  -> mechanism-specific return
  -> cross-return contradiction and residue synthesis
  -> revised bet / collapse-test / witness
  -> evidence harness
```

The default search bound is depth 1 and at most six admitted sources unless a
subtower defines a tighter bound. Source count and cross-subtower repetition are
not confidence or authority measures.

## Residue

- The five deferred-contribution returns are worked examples, not a universal
  proof that every future mechanism needs those same five subtowers.
- Preventive contribution remains a separate bounded sweep and inherits no
  evidence from the completed deferred-contribution cohorts.
- The revised live workshop still needs a trainee cohort before teaching
  effectiveness can be claimed.
