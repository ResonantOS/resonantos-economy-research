## 3. Does "name the owner" make survivors STRONGER (not killed)? — Confirmed.

This is the load-bearing claim of my vector and the confrontation strengthens it. Naming the owner
does three things that *raise* a candidate's standing:

1. **It imports the owner's boundary condition for free** — which is exactly the falsifiability
   Popper wants. "Strategyproofness (Gibbard–Satterthwaite)" doesn't just label L1-INV-A, it *hands
   you the impossibility tax* (only restricted-domain/dictatorial rules achieve it). The owner makes
   the candidate **more** falsifiable, not less.
2. **It de-fuses by provenance** — which is exactly what Quine wants. Two candidates with different
   owners are definitionally distinct *even if they share a lane tag*. Owner-identity is a cleaner
   de-fusion key than tag-adjacency. So "name the owner" is the *operator* that performs Quine's
   de-fuse.
3. **It severs the candidate from the correlated-witness count** — which is my re-rank. Once a row
   says "owner: Douceur 2002," the "four-tower convergence" uplift is visibly redundant and deletes
   itself.

So: **build-from-owned is the opposite of a kill. It is the verdict that makes a candidate
survivable, falsifiable, de-fused, and re-rankable all at once.** Zero of my owned candidates are
killed by the confrontation; several are *strengthened* by gaining a named boundary.
