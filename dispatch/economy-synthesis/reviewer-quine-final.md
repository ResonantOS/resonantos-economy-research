## 2. Where I HOLD the definitional line

### H-1 — HOLD against collapsing my de-fuse into Popper's split. They COMPOSE; they are not identical.

Popper's split would let MERGE-1 *survive as a harness* provided each leg is tagged
decidable-vs-awaits-a-value. That is necessary but **not sufficient** for my vector. Two legs can both be
RULE-DECIDABLE-NOW and still be **different objects that must not share a verdict**. Concretely: imagine
TF-12 someday gets its T\* and becomes decidable. Under Popper alone, decidable-TF-12 and decidable-TF-1
could then ride one MERGE-1 PASS. My objection survives that: conservation (flow-stock) and
centralization (accumulation dynamic) remain **distinct objects** even when both are testable — a ledger
can conserve every epoch while concentrating to one holder. **Decidability does not confer
co-measurability.** So I HOLD: the de-fuse is an *object-identity* requirement that persists independent
of testability status. The consolidated demand must carry **both** the split (axis 2) *and* the de-fuse
(axis 1) — three verdict fields, never one — or it under-determines the fix.

### H-2 — HOLD, sharpened, on the §0.3 affirmative spine. Neither Popper nor Meadows covers this kill.

My hardest initial kill — the move from "four towers locate a *seam*" to "the smallest strengthening
mechanism IS the observe-and-route credit graph" — is untouched by the other two reviewers. Popper's
split is about §6 fixtures; Meadows's re-rank is about §6.2 order; neither reaches the §0/§7 *prose
affirmative*. This is the one place I do not delegate. **HOLD KILL on the affirmative reading.** The
prohibition survives (per-leg violation criteria exist); the design claim does not (un-witnessed,
derived from witnesses §0.1 itself says are correlated). I note Meadows's salience-deletion *reinforces*
this: once you delete salience from the ranking, the affirmative spine has nothing left to stand on but
"convergence," and convergence-as-proof is exactly what §0.1 forbids. So Meadows strengthens my H-2
even though he did not name it.

### H-3 — HOLD: Meadows's "one owned theorem" reframing of Lane-2 #5 STRENGTHENS my de-fuse for MERGE-1 but I must guard it from DISSOLVING the de-fuse where it does NOT apply.

This is the crux the coordinator flagged, so I answer it directly.

**Where it strengthens me (Lane-2 #5):** Meadows says L2-OBLIGATION-A's three legs (failed T4, formal
TF-9, sci TF-1) are **one owned theorem — Douceur Sybil impossibility — restated three times**, hence a
*tautology* dressed as convergence, and should become a single GATE upstream of the build queue. I
**agree entirely**, and note it is the *converse face* of my de-fuse:

- My MERGE-1 attack: **one harness fusing THREE objects** → split into three.
- Meadows's Lane-2 attack: **three table-legs naming ONE object** → collapse into one gate.

These are the same principle (no-distinction-without-a-difference) run in both directions: split where
there are real differences, merge where there are none. Lane-2 #5 has **no difference** across its three
legs (all are "cheap identities defeat per-identity scoring" = Douceur), so they collapse. MERGE-1 has
**three real differences**, so it splits. **Meadows's reframing strengthens, not dissolves, my position
— because it shares my identity criterion and applies it correctly to a case I had not pushed to a single
owner.** I upgrade my W-3: L2-OBLIGATION-A is not merely "residue+constitution not a fixture" (my
initial) — it is **one Douceur gate**, placed upstream, that every per-identity-reward candidate must
clear before entering the queue.

**Where I guard against over-reach (the dissolution risk):** Meadows's "owned theorem" move is *safe
only when the legs are genuinely the same theorem.* It would **dissolve** my de-fuse illegitimately if
applied to MERGE-1 by analogy — i.e. if someone argued "MERGE-1's three legs are all 'Lane-3 emission
health,' one owned theorem, collapse them." They are NOT one theorem: TF-1 is Goodhart/budget-accounting,
TF-12 is Forrester-Meadows accumulation dynamics, TF-7 is Rawlsian distribution. **Three owners, three
objects.** So I HOLD: Meadows's collapse is licensed by *shared owner*, and MERGE-1's legs have *distinct
owners* — which is independent confirmation of my de-fuse. The owner-naming Meadows introduced is in fact
my **strongest new tool**: owner-identity is a decidable test for whether a merge is legitimate. **A merge
is sound iff its legs share an owning theorem; a split is required iff they do not.** Lane-2 #5 shares one
(Douceur) → collapse. MERGE-1 spans three (Goodhart, Forrester-Meadows, Rawls) → split. MERGE-3 shares
one class (randomized-adjudication-vs-coalition) → survives as a co-run. MERGE-2's legs span
Gibbard-Satterthwaite (TF-2) and a distinct estimator-bias property (TF-11) → I now *downgrade* MERGE-2
from my initial SURVIVES toward "two objects kept on one harness only because their *status* is already
separated" (acceptable, but by status-separation, not by shared owner).
