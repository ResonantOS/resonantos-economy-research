## 0. The §0.1 trap — adjudicated first (it governs everything below)

**This is the load-bearing question I was sent to answer, so I answer it before the per-claim table.**

The draft's §0.1 states the correct systems discipline: the four towers are *correlated weak
witnesses* reading **one generative object** (the same un-snapshotted whitepaper + a shared failure
literature), so convergence is "one finding seen N times, not N confirmations." It then declares
convergence is recorded as **SALIENCE, never confidence.** Good. That is exactly the move a
systems thinker owes: do not multiply correlated signals as if independent.

**But the draft does not hold the line it drew.** It self-contradicts in two structural ways:

1. **Salience IS being used as a confidence-shaped quantity in the §6.2 ranking.** The backlog is
   "Ranked by attack-lane leverage × *review survival*," and every keystone box feeds a "very high
   / high salience" tag that pushes items up the list. The draft says salience is only "where to
   look first," but the *ranking is itself the action* — it allocates the scarcest resource in the
   whole program (which fixture gets built next). In systems terms: **salience has been wired into a
   stock-allocation feedback loop.** Once a soft signal sets a set-point for a real resource, the
   "it's only salience" disclaimer is rhetorical, not structural. This is precisely the §0.3 failure
   the draft itself names — *a measurement signal acting as a control authority* — committed by the
   synthesis document about its own measurements. **REVISE: the §0.1 discipline must bind the §6.2
   ranking, not just the prose.** Either rank by *decidability + owner-strength of the single
   strongest leg*, or show that the rank order does not change when every convergence tag is deleted.

2. **Lane-2 #5 (the flagged case) is the cleanest instance of the trap — and it is worse than the
   draft admits.** Lane-2's keystone ("non-transferable ≠ non-farmable; identity-manufacture is the
   underbelly") rests on failed-T4 (journalistic), formal-TF-9 (the draft's *own* words: "local
   inference, not a fetched witness"), and sci-TF-1 (deflationary inference). The draft correctly
   caveats "none is a fetched proof." **But then L2-OBLIGATION-A is ranked #5 and called "high
   leverage."** Here is the systems problem the draft misses: the three legs are not merely
   correlated — **they are the same theorem.** "Per-identity scoring is defeated by cheap
   identities" is a *single* mechanism-design result (the Sybil result, Douceur 2002), restated in
   three vocabularies. TF-9 even says so internally ("IF identity is costless THEN exclusion loses
   its precondition"). So this is not "one finding seen three times" raising salience — it is **one
   owned theorem seen three times, and the convergence adds literally zero**, because all three legs
   inherit the *same* single premise (identity is cheap) and the *same* single owner. Convergence
   across restatements of one theorem is not even weak evidence; it is tautology. **REVISE: Lane-2
   #5's "four-tower salience" claim must be struck and replaced with "one owned result (Sybil
   resistance is impossible without a costly/scarce identity primitive), cited three times."** The
   leverage is real, but it comes from the *owner*, not from the convergence.

**Verdict on the trap: REAL self-contradiction, not a real systems insight — in two places (§6.2
ranking, Lane-2 #5).** The §0.1 framing is correct as written and wrong as practiced. A genuine
systems insight *would* live here, and the draft brushes past it: the right convergence reading is
not "more witnesses ⇒ more salience" but **"convergence tells you the four towers share a leverage
point; go find the single highest leverage point and act there, ignoring the count."** Meadows'
actual teaching is that leverage is about *where* you intervene, and counting witnesses is the
lowest-leverage thing you can do with the observation.

## 2. Adjudicating the synthesizer's 5 flagged weak claims (precedent-relevant ones) + my own finds

The synthesizer flags weak claims at: §0.2 (un-ledgered surface ⇒ analogy cap), TF-12 soft C4 leg,
TF-9 identity-cost residue, socio TF-1 single-study persistence, and the convergence-as-salience
discipline itself. Precedent-relevant adjudication:

- **TF-9 / L2-OBLIGATION-A identity-cost residue (flagged):** **upheld as the largest residue, and I
  raise its severity.** The draft calls the harness "vacuous until an identity-cost model exists."
  Correct — and from the owner (Douceur) we know *why*: Sybil resistance is **impossible** without an
  exogenous costly identity primitive. So this is not a residue to be filled by more research; it is
  a **hard precondition the whitepaper must supply (proof-of-personhood / staked identity / external
  attestation) or the entire per-identity reward surface is undefined.** This is my top demand.
- **socio TF-1 crowding-out persistence single-study (flagged):** **upheld weak.** Owner (Frey/Deci)
  establishes crowding-out *existence* robustly but *persistence* is genuinely contested. The
  within-subject A→B→A' fixture is the right falsification design (Popper will like it). Keep the
  weak tag.
- **TF-12 soft C4 leg / finite-horizon counterpoint (flagged):** **upheld.** Owner-level note: this
  is a real horizon-dependence (Kiayias/Fanti finite-horizon vs Barabási long-run), correctly held
  as competing regimes, not a contradiction. Keep.
- **§0.2 analogy cap (flagged):** **upheld and endorsed** — capping Resonant-specific mappings at
  analogy behind un-ledgered features is the single most disciplined thing the draft does.

**My own finds (precedent/systems vector):**

- **FIND-1 (REVISE) — the §6.2 ranking is driven by an ungoverned salience loop.** See §0. A
  synthesis whose own ranking demonstrates the failure mode it warns against (measurement acting as
  control) must fix this before it can be promoted to "next-stage work order."
- **FIND-2 (REVISE) — Lane-2 #5 convergence is tautological, not corroborative.** See §0. Three
  restatements of one owned theorem (Douceur) sharing one premise (cheap identity). Strike the
  "four-tower salience"; replace with single-owner citation.
- **FIND-3 (build-from-owned, but UNDER-OWNED in the draft) — the §0.3 "separation posture" is
  Ostrom + Beer + Goodhart, fully owned, and the draft presents it as emergent.** The draft says
  "the answer they jointly gesture at." No — Ostrom's design principles already *own* "monitoring
  accountable to the monitored," Beer's VSM owns "audit channel ≠ command channel," and Goodhart owns
  "the measure must not become the target." The draft should stop framing its spine as a corpus
  emergent and cite the three owners. This is not a downgrade — naming the owner makes the posture
  *stronger* and falsifiable, because each owner brings its own boundary condition.
- **FIND-4 (systems-leverage note, not a kill) — the draft mis-ranks leverage.** In Meadows'
  leverage hierarchy, the draft's #1 (TF-11 stratified-estimate fixture) is a *parameter/measurement*
  intervention (low-mid leverage), while L3-INV-C (feedback governor) and the §0.3 separation posture
  (changing the *structure of information flows* and *who has authority over what* — Meadows' leverage
  points #6 and #4) are **higher-leverage interventions.** Ranking the measurement fixture #1 because
  it is "already decidable" optimizes for *decidability*, not *leverage*. That is a legitimate
  triage choice — but the draft should say it is ranking by decidability, not let "leverage" in the
  §6.2 header imply systems leverage. **REVISE the §6.2 header wording or re-rank.**
