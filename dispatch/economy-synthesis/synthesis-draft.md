## 0. Two governing disciplines (read before any lane)

### 0.1 Isolation is not independence — convergence raises SALIENCE, not confidence

The four towers are **isolated witnesses, NOT statistically independent draws.** This holds at
two levels:

1. *Within* a tower, sub-lanes already flagged it: science-of-science (§0/§2: "two corpora
   sharing a generative cause are not two independent draws"); socioeconomic-behavior (§A M-2:
   "three descriptions of motive substitution… never shown to be the same effect in the same
   population").
2. *Across* towers, the same correction binds harder. All four read the **same generative
   object** — credit/reputation systems that pay, under the same un-snapshotted whitepaper. When
   two towers "agree," they often agree because the underlying failure literature is shared
   (e.g. crowding-out appears in socio TF-1, sci TF-9, and as a behavioral import inside failed
   T5). **That is one finding seen three times, not three confirmations.**

Therefore, throughout this map: **cross-tower convergence is recorded as SALIENCE (where to look
first / what owes a fixture), never as a confidence multiplier and never as proof.** Where a
convergence would have to lean on a soft/inference/single-study leg, it is tagged and earns no
uplift. This is the single discipline that keeps the synthesis from manufacturing certainty out
of correlated witnesses.

### 0.2 The whitepaper is un-snapshotted — surface assertions stay operator-reading

The ledger (C1–C6) verifies only: Phase-1 contribution-first (C1), anti-capital posture (C2),
contextual non-1t1v governance (C3), `$RCT` non-transferable (C4), `$RES` transferable /
treasury-owned / external-liquidity-later (C5), Phase-2 financial mechanisms gated/future (C6).

The ledger does **NOT** carry — so this synthesis may not treat as locally verified — the
following un-ledgered surface assertions, all **operator-reading** until they land in
`claims/SOURCE-CLAIM-LEDGER.md`:
**1B `$RES` supply · buyback-burn as live present policy · named `$R10/$R12/$R15` instruments ·
six-sector `$RCT` structure · named custodian-bootstrap phase · AI-agent-sponsor rule.**

Every mechanism whose collapse-test rides on one of these is capped at **analogy** and **gated**
behind that feature landing in the ledger. The *precedent* stays real; the *Resonant mapping*
drops to analogy. (This is failed-DAO §0 generalized to the whole corpus, and it is consistent
with formal TF-13's "thresholds are parameters, not findings" and sci's RES-SoS-5 keystone.)

### 0.3 The dialectic framing question — answered as a PROHIBITION (reviewer-round result)

From `TWO-LANE-DIALECTICS.md`: **what is the smallest mechanism that STRENGTHENS an institution
rather than replacing it?**

The reviewer round **killed the affirmative existence-claim** the draft previously made here —
*"the smallest strengthening mechanism IS the observe-and-route credit graph"* (Quine KILL,
co-signed by Popper). That affirmative forbids no observation, is derived from witnesses §0.1
itself calls correlated, and is therefore unfalsifiable from this corpus. It is struck. What
survives:

> **PROHIBITION (survives — falsifiable per-leg).** Measurement (reputation / contribution / audit
> signal) must NOT act as authority, as payout set-point, or as governance weight. This forbids an
> observable: it is falsified by exhibiting a working system in which measurement DOES act as
> authority *safely*. Per-leg violation criteria exist (formal TF-2 `∂authority/∂self_report=0`;
> the flow-budget law; reviewer⊥reviewed credit), so the prohibition has FAIL-regions.

Three of four towers locate the central failure at this seam (formal's "measurement signal acting
as control authority"; sci's "any paying metric rots / gatekeeper who evaluates-and-benefits";
socio's "price/control signal reframes the act"). That observation is **salience / prose
annotation only, never proof** (§0.1) — and it may not order anything.

**Reviewer dissent carried (Meadows HOLDS, against Quine/Popper — preserved verbatim in §8):** the
affirmative form — *a separated observing-and-routing function strengthens an institution* —
survives **NOT as a corpus finding but as a BUILD-FROM-OWNED design posture**, licensed by
precedent (owner: **Ostrom** design principle of monitoring accountable to the monitored + **Beer**
VSM System-3* audit channel distinct from System-3 command + **separation-of-powers** tradition),
each with its own boundary. This is a license from *precedent*, not from this corpus.

### Candidate mechanisms / invariants (Lane 1)

- **L1-INV-A — Self-report independence of authority.** `∂authority/∂self_report = 0` (formal
  TF-2). The strongest, most decidable Lane-1 invariant. It is **blocked pending specification**
  (the whitepaper prose "governance influence *should* depend on contribution/reputation/recent
  activity" is underspecified on binding-allocation vs non-binding-routing — it cannot be
  certified violated, only flagged).
- **L1-INV-B — Estimate/authority arm separation.** A measurement loop must not become authority
  (formal TF-11). This is the formal expression of the §0.3 **prohibition** (sole NOW
  promotion-candidate; adversary-class pending, §8).
- **L1-INV-C — Reviewer-credit ⊥ reviewed-credit** (sci TF-5). The same seam as L1-INV-A/B, seen
  from the evaluator-benefit side. Magnitude (OR=3.5, 1-in-5) stays quarantined as academic.
- **L1-RESIDUE — quorum / proposal-right / payload-immutability / custodian-transition gaps**
  (failed T6, T8, T7). These are *documented absences*, recorded as residue (maps to claim
  residue R1, R3, R4), never mechanisms.

### Cross-tower CONVERGENCE (salience, not confidence)

> **Lane-1 keystone convergence: "the credit/measurement signal must be structurally barred from
> acting as authority."** Formal TF-2/TF-11/TF-4, sci TF-5, and (by exit-behavior) socio
> TF-8/TF-9 all land here from four different method bases (order-morphism algebra; stratified
> estimation; impossibility theorem; academic gatekeeper data; exit dynamics).

This is the highest-salience cross-tower agreement in the entire corpus. **But by §0.1 it is one
seam witnessed four ways, not four independent proofs** — formal's two legs (Fritz F2 + Beer F2)
are the only *clean* formal convergence (C1); sci TF-5's DAO mapping is analogy; socio TF-8's EVL
core is analogy. The agreement says *look here first*; it does not certify the invariant.

### Cross-tower TENSION (genuine, preserve)

- **T-L1.1 — "separate the signal from authority" vs "no clean signal exists to separate."**
  Formal TF-2 says: route reputation onto a display/routing edge, keep `∂authority/∂self_report=0`
  and you are safe. Sci TF-1 says: the routing signal you fall back on (downstream usage / fork
  graph) is *itself a payable metric* and inherits Goodhart. **Formal's separation cure assumes a
  trustworthy independent input exists; sci denies one is free.** Unresolved. The reconciliation
  is not "pick one" — it is that L1-INV-A is necessary but not sufficient: separating self-report
  from authority does nothing if the "independent" input is also farmed (this is exactly failed
  T4's identity-manufacture argument arriving in Lane 1).
- **T-L1.2 — randomness as defense vs illegibility (Dialectic C).** Formal TF-10 wants
  unpredictable audit selection to defend the audit *signal*; socio TF-3 warns opaque/random
  allocation erodes legitimacy when a high-claim contributor loses. Reconciliation the towers
  jointly support: **randomness belongs in audit/reviewer-assignment selection (formal), NOT in
  reward allocation (socio).** This is the cleanest cross-tower agreement on *where* randomness
  enters — and it directly answers Dialectic-C's synthesis question.
