## Agent 1 - Ostrom, Elinor

Read-only route completed. No files edited. Claim posture: CAv2 is supporting precedent for method and boundary design only, not ResonantOS economy authority, validation, token/payout policy, or implementation readiness.

**Findings**

1. **Lifecycle primitives**
CAv2 separates lifecycle into distinct fields: `kind`, `status`, `evidence`, `commitment`, `provenance`, `owner`, and `residue`; no field may silently imply another. See [DEFINITIONS.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/definitions/DEFINITIONS.md:1321) and [DEFINITIONS.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/definitions/DEFINITIONS.md:1340).
Transferable primitive: economy research can use this as a field-separation discipline for research statuses, credit signals, reward effects, and authority effects.

2. **Owner-route discipline**
CAv2's central rule is that authority moves by owner route, not usefulness, proximity, or repetition. Candidate material must be routed to the narrowest responsible owner before it governs work. See [AUTHORITY-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/AUTHORITY-MODEL.md:23) and [AUTHORITY-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/AUTHORITY-MODEL.md:90).
Ostrom-style transferable read: this is institutional boundary work, not a reward mechanism. It names who can decide a movement, what gate applies, and what remains outside that owner's authority.

3. **Promotion lifecycle**
CAv2's promotion path is staged: source evidence, candidate, owner-routed review, promoted authority, and optional invariant/constitution pressure, with gates and stop/demotion rules. See [DEFINITIONS.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/definitions/DEFINITIONS.md:3189). The local policy instantiates this as source evidence through stable repository asset, but says the ladder is not automatic. See [promotion-policy.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/promotion-policy.md:8) and [AUTHORITY-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/AUTHORITY-MODEL.md:31).
Transferable primitive: use staged research movement plus explicit demotion/stop rules before any economy recommendation escalates.

4. **Ontology non-collapse boundaries**
CAv2 ontology can model authority and prepare promotion requests, but cannot promote itself or redefine definitions, constitutions, specs, runtime contracts, schemas, movement edges, or public claims. See [ontology/README.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/ontology/README.md:12).
The lifecycle model makes the verdict hole explicit: ontology can represent the rail/projection, but the owner profile produces the verdict. See [LIFECYCLE-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/ontology/LIFECYCLE-MODEL.md:38).
Transferable primitive: economy ontology/inventory may organize evidence and candidate meanings, but must not become policy, reward eligibility, or authority by generated mirror.

5. **Node/relation guardrails**
CAv2 candidate nodes must carry source refs, owner route, candidate status, challenge contract, and forbidden collapse. See [NODE-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/ontology/NODE-MODEL.md:12). Candidate relations must name endpoints, direction, owner route, evidence, effect, and forbidden collapse. See [RELATION-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/ontology/RELATION-MODEL.md:13).
Blocked relation uses are explicit: movement edges, ledger events as subgraphs, and promotion events as nodes require schema governance, edge law, and authority-ledger validation first. See [RELATION-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/ontology/RELATION-MODEL.md:60).

6. **Closed authority-kind boundary**
CAv2 default-denies unenumerated authority kinds until an owner decision adds the kind with lifecycle and owner route. See [DEFINITIONS.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/definitions/DEFINITIONS.md:3245) and [AUTHORITY-MODEL.md](/home/vrondelli/projects/domainspec-core/cyberAlchemy-v2/authority/AUTHORITY-MODEL.md:63).
Transferable primitive: if economy research invents a new authority-bearing category, it should be blocked until the local authority model names its owner route and lifecycle.
