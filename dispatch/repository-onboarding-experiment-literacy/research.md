### Recommended teaching sequence

1. **Begin with a 60–90 second situation**, before vocabulary: “A community wants to recognize useful mentoring, but fears people will optimize for visible mentoring records.”
2. **Ask one pre-question:** “What result would convince you the rule helps, and what result would make you drop it?”
3. **Reveal the lifecycle as six objects:** prize → bet → experiment → fixture → run → verdict.
4. **Show one fully worked example**, including a failing fixture and an invalid run—not only a successful path.
5. **Explain fixtures immediately:** a fixture is a known-input rehearsal for the evidence machinery. It can show that records, rejection rules, and calculations behave as declared; it cannot show that the mechanism helps real people.
6. **Give a second, partially completed example** in a different domain.
7. **Give a contrasting failure example:** fixture passes, but the live experiment fails or is invalid.
8. **Ask the reader to complete a third example unaided.**
9. **Run a retell and boundary check** before sending them into the full inventory and canonical documents.

Progressive disclosure should expose three doors after the short introduction:

- “Understand the lifecycle.”
- “Create a first experiment.”
- “Inspect the repository’s current evidence.”

The inventory remains the canonical start surface; this should not become a competing authority index.

### Example families

Use different surface stories while preserving the same research structure:

- **Mentorship recognition:** Does bounded mentoring evidence improve correct recognition without generating logging theater?
- **Software maintenance:** Does recognizing preventive maintenance reduce recurring failures without encouraging low-value activity inflation?
- **Newcomer access:** Does a qualified lottery broaden access without randomizing eligibility, safety, or authority?
- **Review process:** Does adding a second independent reviewer reduce capture without unacceptable delay?

At least one example should end `FALSIFIED`, one `INVALID`, and one still `OPEN`. Otherwise newcomers may mistake the lifecycle for a machine that manufactures survived verdicts.

### Analogy constraints

Recommended, with explicit breakpoints:

- **Fixture = crash-test dummy for the evidence pipeline.** Mapping: known setup, expected failure, instrument check. Break: a fixture contains no real community behavior and proves no general safety.
- **Bet = prediction card, not casino wager.** Mapping: committed claim plus failure condition. Break: no money, stake, status, or authority is transferred.
- **Prize = open research finish line, not bounty.** Mapping: a bounded question and closure witness. Break: closure authorizes no payout or implementation.
- **Verdict = result under declared match rules.** Mapping: scoped adjudication. Break: it is not universal truth or policy permission.

Never let the analogy become the definition; follow each with the canonical operational term.

### Likely misconceptions to probe

- “Prize” means payment.
- “Bet” means economic staking.
- The mechanism is assumed true while only its wording is tested.
- A schema or fixture pass means the mechanism works.
- One successful bet proves community utility and communication safety.
- `SURVIVED` means deployable.
- `INVALID` means the conjecture was falsified.
- A metric or recognition signal grants authority.
- An attractive story counts as causal evidence.

### Comprehension witness

Require the newcomer to:

1. Retell the six-object lifecycle without looking.
2. Explain why a fixture pass is not a live-experiment verdict.
3. Given a novel scenario, produce one prize, two independent bets, a comparator, falsifier, fixture pair, and scoped verdict template.
4. Correctly classify evidence as source, route, fixture, live-run, or promotion evidence.
5. Identify what a survived verdict still cannot authorize.

Survival should require both accurate retelling and successful transfer. Reading satisfaction or term recall alone is insufficient.

## Agent 3 — Myerson, Roger

## Explorer return — exact experiment mechanics

### 1. Lifecycle map

```text
story or mechanism idea
  → prize
  → bet
  → frozen experiment criterion
  → synthetic fixture validation
  → separately approved run
  → captured evidence
  → scoped verdict
  → typed negative/residue or owner review
```

1. **Start with the desired observable change.** The local walkthrough explicitly begins by asking what would have to become observably better, not by designing balances or payouts (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:37-55`).

2. **Prize = bounded question, not purse.** Minimum fields are: ID, target/question, current evidence state, required witness, award collapse-test, allowed/blocked use, and status (`prizes/README.md:35-45`). It closes only when a candidate survives its attack and authority-collapse check with a witness; novelty is irrelevant (`prizes/README.md:58-71`).

3. **Bet = mechanism conjecture with a way to lose.** Minimum fields are: ID, prize, conjecture, comparator, predicted observation, falsifier, attack lane, and open resolution. Comparator, observation, and falsifier must exist before the run (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:183-219`; canonical register fields at `prizes/README.md:47-56`). It is never an economic stake (`prizes/BET-REGISTER.md:6-10`).

4. **Experiment proposal = freeze the adjudication rule before results.** The experiment dispatch proposes, validity-checks, and freezes `criterion.md`; it does not run the experiment (`.agents/skills/domainspec-experiment-dispatch/SKILL.md:16-21,55-70`). The criterion must contain one hypothesis, non-goals, falsification condition, mechanical verdict rule, discrimination check, and preregistered categories (`ibid.:72-96`). A practical witness also pins unit, population, comparator, assignment, outcome, guardrails, thresholds, exclusions, missing-data policy, and verdict rules (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:336-363`).

5. **Fixtures = prove the measuring apparatus before trusting a measurement.** At minimum: schema, one synthetic passing record, one synthetic failing record, deterministic calculations, and a status-safe result template (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:375-400`). The harness requires passing and failing fixtures, preserved dry-run/live separation, and no claim upgrade from synthetic data (`.agents/skills/research-evidence-harness/SKILL.md:68-85,88-101`). The local contribution fixture schema demonstrates the useful minimum: input, observed signal, review action, expected state, forbidden mutations, typed negative, deterministic check, human check, owner route, blocked escalation, and residue (`validation/contribution-edge-fixtures/SCHEMA.md:6-31`).

6. **Run = separate downstream activity.** The frozen criterion becomes read-only; the later runner produces raw `experiment.md`, and the adjudicator produces `findings.md` from criterion plus result (`.agents/skills/domainspec-experiment-dispatch/SKILL.md:142-158,167-179`).

7. **Evidence layers stay separate.** Sources establish precedent, fixtures establish record/calculation behavior, a live experiment establishes observations only under declared conditions, review finds flaws, and promotion is an owner decision (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:404-426`).

8. **Verdict = scoped comparison to frozen criteria.** Record bet ID, verdict, evidence references, criterion-by-criterion comparison, population/conditions, typed negative, claim change, residue, and next owner (`ibid.:430-468`). `INVALID` means the protocol cannot adjudicate; it is not the same as `FALSIFIED` (`.agents/skills/domainspec-experiment-dispatch/SKILL.md:98-127`).

### 2. What may be simplified

Useful newcomer analogies:

- **Prize:** a research question with a finish condition, not a bag of money.
- **Bet:** a claim that writes down how it could lose, not a financial wager.
- **Frozen criterion:** a sealed envelope containing the finish line before the race.
- **Fixtures:** crash-test dummies for the evidence pipeline—they show whether the sensors work, not whether real passengers are safe.
- **Verdict:** a match report for one declared field and date, not a license to deploy.

The teaching sequence, sample YAML, generic IDs, thresholds, and narrative vocabulary may be simplified because the existing walkthrough already treats them as non-authoritative teaching aids (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.validation.md:65-79`). Vocabulary can arrive after the reader feels the problem (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:59-73`).

Do **not** simplify away:

- the comparator or falsifier;
- the distinction between `FALSIFIED` and `INVALID`;
- independent bets for independent causal promises;
- fixture versus live evidence;
- frozen thresholds and exclusions;
- source, research, and policy authority boundaries;
- the rule that narrative analogy never writes operational consequences (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:251-270`).

### 3. Three complete synthetic example families

#### A. Community event: invisible setup work

- **Starting story:** A community gathering succeeds. The speaker is visible, while someone who found the venue, handled accessibility, and followed up with absent members disappears from the recap.
- **Prize:** Can bounded pre-event evidence improve correct recognition of enabling work without crediting attendance or self-description alone?
- **Bet/hypothesis:** For the same event cases, reviewers given bounded activity evidence identify more reference-labeled enabling contributions than reviewers given the public recap alone.
- **Comparison:** Same invented cases, blinded and counterbalanced: recap-only versus recap-plus-evidence.
- **Fixture:** A synthetic case set contains true enabling work, attendance-only, self-asserted but uncorroborated work, and irrelevant prior activity. One valid run row contains all assignment/outcome fields; one invalid row omits assignment; one dangerous-success case accepts attendance as contribution.
- **Expected observation:** Preregistered genuine-link recovery improves while false acceptance, disagreement, review time, and entitlement errors stay below frozen bounds.
- **Falsifier:** No meaningful recovery gain, or any gain is erased by false attribution or burden.
- **Invalidity:** Reviewers see different case sets; reference labels are authored after reviewer output; assignment is missing; evidence packets reveal the expected answer.
- **Cannot prove:** That recognition increases future participation, justifies rewards, or generalizes beyond the declared cases.

#### B. Code review and maintenance: visibility is not value

- **Starting story:** A feature author gets attention. A maintainer quietly catches a breaking migration, reviews dependencies, and prevents an incident.
- **Prize:** Can structured maintenance evidence identify risk-reducing work better than commit-count or merged-PR visibility without turning visibility into authority?
- **Bet/hypothesis:** Reviewers using a bounded maintenance record correctly classify more independently seeded risk-reducing work than reviewers using commit and merge counts.
- **Comparison:** Identical synthetic repository histories: activity-count dashboard versus evidence packets with review context.
- **Fixture:** Include a low-volume high-impact review, high-volume cosmetic commits, self-approved changes, reciprocal-review farming, and a legitimate feature. Passing fixture preserves review and all reward/authority effects as `none`; failing fixture automatically upgrades high activity to credit or merge authority. This mirrors the repository’s rejected transitions (`validation/contribution-edge-fixtures/TYPED-NEGATIVES.md:10-18`).
- **Expected observation:** Higher correct classification with no increase in false positives, reviewer capture, or review-time bound.
- **Falsifier:** Counts perform equally well, or structured evidence creates self-report farming, capture, or unacceptable burden.
- **Invalidity:** Reviewers know the seeded labels; one arm receives richer task outcomes; definitions change mid-run; the contributor is sole witness.
- **Cannot prove:** Improved long-term software quality, safe automatic scoring, reward eligibility, merge rights, or governance power.

#### C. Newcomer mentorship: story versus terminology

- **Starting story:** A newcomer opens the repository, meets “prize,” “bet,” and “fixture,” and assumes money, gambling, and live testing. A mentor instead tells a concrete story before naming the objects.
- **Prize:** Can story-first onboarding improve correct experiment construction without introducing payout or authority misconceptions?
- **Bet/hypothesis:** Newcomers receiving a story-first explanation produce more structurally complete experiment cards than newcomers receiving a terminology-first checklist, without more entitlement errors.
- **Comparison:** Same unfamiliar synthetic mechanism prompt; story-first guide versus vocabulary-first guide.
- **Fixture:** Synthetic reader responses include: complete prize/bet/witness cards; prize-as-payout; fixture-pass-as-mechanism-proof; one bet inheriting another’s evidence; and a response with missing comparator. Validator must accept only the structurally complete, boundary-safe record.
- **Expected observation:** More readers independently produce a prize, at least two separate bets, a comparator, frozen witness, and correct evidence-layer boundary, while payout/authority errors remain below the preregistered bound.
- **Falsifier:** Completion does not improve, or story language increases prize-as-reward, fixture-as-live-proof, or evidence-inheritance errors.
- **Invalidity:** Prior harness experience is imbalanced; mentors intervene differently; scoring rubric changes after reading outputs; evaluators know condition assignment.
- **Cannot prove:** Mentorship effectiveness generally, community retention, mechanism utility, or any economy outcome. The repository already declares its present newcomer trial unrun and teaching effectiveness flagged (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.validation.md:19-25,106-125`).

### 4. Primary failure analysis

- Treating the prize as a reward converts research into bounty logic.
- Treating the mechanism as assumed truth leaves only copy or implementation details “testable.”
- Letting one bet inherit another’s evidence confuses attribution, social effect, and communication safety (`prizes/RESEARCH-HARNESS-BETS-PRIZES-WALKTHROUGH.md:223-241`).
- Passing synthetic fixtures can prove schema behavior while proving nothing about people (`ibid.:375-400`).
- Moving thresholds after results destroys preregistration.
- Calling protocol failure “falsification” hides an uninformative run.
- A positive result without population, duration, and exclusions becomes universalized fiction.
- A score, visibility signal, or local research pass must never become reward or authority (`authority/AUTHORITY-MODEL.md:75-87`; `definitions/DEFINITIONS.md:565-600`).
- Even a survived bet only becomes a candidate for owner review; research cannot authorize policy, payout, governance, or implementation (`authority/AUTHORITY-MODEL.md:20-37`).
