## Six Decisions And Result-Link Dispositions

The integration must expose six separate decisions:

1. describe an act;
2. forecast a plan;
3. adjudicate one bounded result-link claim;
4. allocate one future opportunity under an independent procedure;
5. make no reward decision here; and
6. make no authority decision here.

`Accepted` means only that one submitted claim passed the frozen evidence and
review criteria for one named result. In a synthetic fixture it means agreement
with a seeded fixture link class. It does not mean causal truth, overall value,
merit, future qualification, or community utility. It may create at most one
non-aggregable result-scoped record.

`Rejected` means the evidence contradicts the claim, does not support the
declared link class, or places it outside the admissible claim. The underlying
act description may remain; no result-scoped record is created.

`Held` means conflict, missing provenance, competing accounts, privacy limits,
or material uncertainty prevents acceptance or rejection. A hold needs a
reason, timestamp, expiry, required new evidence, re-review owner, and criteria
version. Expiry closes the hold with no result-scoped record and no automatic
acceptance; reopening requires a new versioned review.

Every disposition retains reward, payout, reputation, governance, and authority
effects as `none` (`dispatch/deferred-contribution-basis/findings.md`;
`robot-talks.md`).

## Revised Experiment Decomposition

### A - Description, Planning, And Review

Kill the omnibus `vectors are better` claim. Split A into:

- **A-description:** test contributor-confirmed multi-role descriptions on
  seeded omitted, honorary, hybrid, invisible, and joint-role cases.
  Confirmation proves declaration provenance only. CRediT owns the description
  component; the local comparison effect is unwitnessed (`research.md`, Agent 2).
- **A-planning:** compare a planning vector with a scalar/reference comparator
  for forecast error, omitted-task displacement, burden, and subgroup error.
  Effort and difficulty remain planning-only. Holmstrom-Milgrom owns the
  multitask-distortion model, not the local dimensions (`research.md`, Agent 3;
  Agent 4).
- **A-review:** test unscalarized dossiers on seeded result-link classes, false
  credit, omissions, ordering reversals, joint-dependency preservation,
  disagreement, and burden. Shapley Flow owns conditional attribution under a
  supplied DAG, not live genuine-link truth (`research.md`, Agent 3).

### B - Review Integrity

Split B into conflict discovery, recusal, current opportunity-specific
qualification, status masking/review, assignment from one frozen pool, and
disposition/appeal. NIH, Tomkins, and Jecmen own different bounded components;
none owns the integrated effect. Conflict-free does not imply qualified,
history may trigger but cannot satisfy a current check, masking is not a
universal remedy, and randomized assignment does not discover hidden conflicts
or create fairness (`research.md`, Agents 2-3).

### C - Gate/Access And Provisional Filtered Lottery

Rename `qualified lottery` to `provisional filtered lottery` and split:

- **C1 gate/access:** test current capability, availability, workload,
  conflict, safety, newcomer/resource access, and gate error. No admitted owner
  establishes a valid or fair local gate.
- **C2 filtered-draw operation:** after a frozen gate, compare auditable random
  order with history ranking or rotation. HRC/Liu owns an operated
  threshold-then-draw precedent and applicant perceptions in that setting, not

If C1 fails, C2 may still test RNG, probability, reroll, and concentration

## CM-Q6 Consequence Activation

CM-Q6 is a locally proposed integration. Its arms become:

1. **shadow/private:** record available only to the research procedure, with no
   public visibility or scarce benefit;
2. **visible-status:** public or reviewer-visible record with no formal scarce
   benefit; measure attention, prestige, perceived rank, and status; and
3. **scarce-benefit:** hold visibility constant from arm 2 and activate one
   bounded experimental opportunity consequence.

The arm 2-to-3 comparison only imperfectly isolates formal benefit activation;
arm 1-to-2 separately measures visibility/status. Measure gaming, crowding,
exclusion, estimate drift, record splitting, attrition, distribution,
legitimacy, and implicit rank. Reward, payout, reputation-system, governance,
and authority effects remain `none` (`research.md`, Agent 4;
`robot-talks.md`).

## Witness Sequence

1. A **seeded-fixture witness** tests internal classification, field
   completeness, conflicts, no-effect transitions, and hold expiry on
   constructed link classes. It does not establish field truth.
2. An **adjudication-robustness witness** compares criteria versions,
   alternative DAGs, independent reviewers, masking, order, appeal, and
   disagreement. Reviewer convergence is not truth.
3. A **longitudinal feedback witness** attacks `history visibility ->
   review/opportunity access -> visible results -> incumbent/reviewer status ->
   rule/weight/defense control -> more visibility` and inspects storage, query,
   UI, reporting, and decision use for implicit totals or de facto rank.

No stage alone supplies an integrated-effect witness (`robot-talks.md`).
