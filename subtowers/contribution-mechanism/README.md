# Contribution Mechanism Research Tower

Status: bounded research dispatch closed; review unregistered and awaiting refreshed confirmation

Validation state: no mechanism experiment has run

## Purpose

This tower studies the whole contribution mechanism discussed in the workshop:

```text
contribution types
  -> dimensions and evidence
  -> witnessed prior contribution records
  -> later result-link claim
  -> review and realization
  -> opportunity allocation and learning
```

Goodhart pressure, conflict, nepotism, failure farming, cumulative advantage,
and authority collapse are mandatory attack lenses across that chain. They are
not the tower's only subject.

The tower must keep four decisions separate:

1. describing a contribution;
2. planning it;
3. reviewing its connection to one witnessed result; and
4. allocating a future contribution or review opportunity.

It studies contribution families, unscalarized dimensions, investment and
realization language, informative failures, reviewer integrity, history,
qualified lotteries, newcomer access, the existing bets and prizes, and the
experiments needed to falsify candidate mechanisms.

## Prior Decision Boundary

The accepted deferred-contribution dispatch rejected `investment` and
`cashing` as already-defined mechanism aliases. The bounded research preserves
and sharpens that verdict: `investment`, `realization`, and `cashing` remain
KILL as mechanism fields. They may appear only as stimuli in the independent
`BET-PRZ-06-a` communication-safety test, paired with operational language:

- `contribution investment` -> witnessed prior contribution record whose later
  relevance remains unproven;
- `realization` or `cashing` -> a contestable result-link review, with no
  automatic monetary, reward, reputation, governance, or authority effect.

The open test asks whether that bilingual explanation improves comprehension or
instead creates entitlement and financial-promise confusion. It cannot validate
the mechanism or resolve the other two bets.

## Research Tracks

| Track | Central question |
| --- | --- |
| Contribution ontology | Which descriptive families cover engineering, maintenance, governance, community care, learning, communication, and collective sensemaking without ranking them on one ladder? |
| Dimensions and estimation | Which planning and result dimensions are useful, and which comparisons become invalid across families, teams, or decisions? |
| Investment and realization | When may a witnessed prior contribution support a later result-specific claim, and what must block automatic accumulation or entitlement? |
| Informative failure | When does a negative result reduce uncertainty or prevent repeated effort, and how is failure farming detected? |
| Review integrity | Which conflict, identity, qualification, random-assignment, appeal, and audit controls reduce nepotism or reciprocal review? |
| Opportunity allocation | How do history-ranked, qualified-lottery, rotation, and newcomer-access procedures affect quality, concentration, safety, and legitimacy? |
| Bets and experiments | Which existing or candidate conjectures are falsifiable, what evidence do they require, and what would kill them? |
| Goodhart pressure | What changes when a proxy begins controlling recognition, eligibility, allocation, or a prize, and does the defense create another target? |
