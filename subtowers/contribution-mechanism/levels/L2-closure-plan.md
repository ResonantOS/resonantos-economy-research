## Stage 3 — Review Dispatch

Next: refresh the proposed review sheet against the artifacts that now exist,
rerun the tension gate if the target changes materially, and seek a separate
human confirmation. The review must attack:

- fidelity to source, authority, and the prior demotion;
- citation, ownership, and definition integrity;
- operability and experiment readiness; and
- abuse, Goodhart pressure, conflict, nepotism, concentration, failure farming,
  and newcomer harm.

The review emits verified change requests. It does not apply fixes or validate
the mechanism.

## Stage 4 — Evidence Handoff

Only reviewed, source-backed experiment candidates may be prepared for an
evidence harness. Each handoff must freeze:

- objective and proxy;
- arms and assignment procedure;
- inclusion, conflict, and safety boundaries;
- primary and distributional measures;
- invalidity conditions;
- collapse threshold;
- data minimization and appeal route; and
- explicit `none` effects for reward, payout, reputation, governance, and
  authority.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and preserve residue if:

- the investment/cashing language cannot avoid entitlement or financial-promise
  confusion;
- dimensions require hidden weights or person totals to function;
- review independence depends on an unchallengeable reputation hierarchy;
- a randomness proposal cannot state what it protects and what it must not
  randomize;
- useful-failure recognition cannot distinguish new evidence from cheap repeat;
- an allocation rule produces an unbounded history-to-opportunity feedback loop;
- a Goodhart defense only renames the optimized target; or
- the next step would require policy or authority movement.
