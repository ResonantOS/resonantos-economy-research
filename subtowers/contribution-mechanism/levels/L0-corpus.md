## External Candidate Cohorts

The proposed research dispatch decides admission. Listing a candidate here does
not establish that its conclusions transfer to a DAO.

### Metric pressure and incentive distortion

- Charles Goodhart, *Problems of Monetary Management: The U.K. Experience*
  (1975) — original monetary-control context.
- Donald T. Campbell, *Assessing the Impact of Planned Social Change* (1976) —
  social-indicator corruption under decision pressure.
- Marilyn Strathern, *Improving Ratings: Audit in the British University
  System* (1997) — audit systems and target displacement.
- David Manheim and Scott Garrabrant, *Categorizing Variants of Goodhart's Law*
  (2018) — regressional, extremal, causal, and adversarial variants.
- Bengt Holmstrom and Paul Milgrom, *Multitask Principal-Agent Analyses* (1991)
  — distortion when measured and unmeasured tasks compete.
- [Wikipedia: Goodhart's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goodhart%27s_law) —
  orientation and bibliography only; never an evidence authority.

### Estimation, review, failure, and allocation

- Scrum Guide 2020 — local/domain sizing and planning precedent.
- NIH peer-review conflict-of-interest policy — disclosure and recusal
  precedent.
- Tomkins, Zhang, and Heavlin (2017) — identity and prestige in peer review.
- Jecmen et al. (2023) — evaluation of randomized peer-review assignment.
- New Zealand Health Research Council Explorer Grants — eligibility followed by
  randomized ordering as operating precedent.
- Madsen and Desai (2010), Franco et al. (2014), and Packalen and Bhattacharya
  (2019) — organizational learning from failure, null-result visibility, and
  newcomer/experience analogies.

## Search Bound

- One depth-1 web pass per external lane.
- At most six admitted sources per lane.
- At least one negative or mechanism-attacking precedent per lane, or a
  documented bounded no-result.
- No recursive bibliography chase; cited-but-unfetched sources become residue.
- Surplus candidates become named residue rather than hidden selection.
- Every source records source kind, supported claim, unsupported inference,
  local bridge, and disanalogy.
