### TF-1 — The downstream-credit cure and the Goodhart warning collide (central tension #1) — SURVIVES

- **Statement:** Lane 1 (B) says credit is community-set by downstream citation and self/equal-declared credit distorts; lane 2 (F1) says any numeric score stops measuring contribution the moment it gates reward. An on-chain "downstream usage / who-forks-depends-on-reuses" graph is *itself a payable metric*, so it does not escape Goodhart — it relocates the gaming surface from self-declaration to manufactured dependency/forks. There is no ungameable downstream signal to borrow.
- **Source-kind:** local-inference (deflationary — it removes a safe-metric claim, adds no DAO measurement; neither source states the conjunction).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** Choose ResonantDAO's credit primitive — self/peer-declared weights (gameable directly) vs. independent downstream-usage graph (gameable via manufactured dependency/forks). Whichever is chosen, name the anti-gaming witness BEFORE the signal can touch `$RCT` or `$RES`.
- **Pressure point:** ER-R2, ER-R3, ER-R4. Control `observed-signal-not-credit`.
- **Lanes:** Lane 2, Lane 3, Lane 1.
- **Verdict:** **borrow-carefully (warning-pair) / block-the-safe-metric-claim.** This finding does NOT license a fork-graph credit primitive.
- **Per-finding collapse-test:** if a ResonantDAO credit signal is ever observed to remain accurate *after* it gates reward (no measurable optimization-against), TF-1's tension dissolves and the chosen primitive may be borrowed. Until then, the safe-metric claim is blocked.

### TF-2 — Priority-as-settlement manufactures disputes — DEMOTED TO ANALOGY-ONLY

- **Statement:** In academia, one property right (priority/being first) carrying all reward-affect makes disputes "frequent, harsh, ugly" (Merton 1957). Whether mapping first-to-submit / first-commit / first-bounty-claim on-chain imports the *same* dispute structure is **unestablished**: on-chain timestamps are cryptographically/deterministically settled, whereas academic priority was socially contested. This **disanalogy is unexamined** and must be engaged before any borrow.
- **Source-kind:** related-source (Merton, academic antecedent) + analogy (on-chain mapping — unbridged).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** Does ResonantDAO treat first-attribution as THE settlement rule or as one signal among several? AND does deterministic on-chain timestamping dissolve, relocate, or merely rename the academic priority dispute?
- **Pressure point:** ER-R1, ER-R4.
- **Lanes:** Lane 1, Lane 4.
- **Verdict:** **analogy-only** (skeptic DEMOTE — priority shares a *word*, not a demonstrated mechanism; deterministic-timestamp disanalogy unaddressed). Survives only as a named question.

### TF-3 — Simultaneous/independent contribution needs a tie rule — future-work (attribution corrected)

- **Statement:** Multiple/simultaneous discovery is recurrent in science (Merton); a winner-take-all scheme over a fixed target invites duplicate effort and ex-post tie disputes. A tie-window + tie-rule is **bounty hygiene reachable from first principles** for any posted bounty with a spec and a clock — it is *consistent with* Merton's multiple-discovery observation but **not evidenced by it** (lab discovery months apart with no shared spec is not the same object as concurrent submissions to a posted bounty).
- **Source-kind:** local-inference (first-principles bounty hygiene); Merton cited as *consistent-with*, NOT as proof.
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** For near-identical independent submissions inside a window — winner-take-all, split, or co-priority? Define tie-window and rule before any bounty is live.
- **Pressure point:** ER-R1.
- **Lanes:** Lane 4, Lane 1.
- **Verdict:** **future-work** (Lane 4 is post-Lanes-1–3). Merton is not cited as evidence of an on-chain tie problem.

### TF-4 — Reward concentration starves maintenance/care unless verification is first-class paid (requirement-residue, NOT promoted)

Shared implication: unless verification, maintenance, moderation, and care work are first-class *rewarded acts*, they remain an unpaid externality and concentration compounds.
- **Source-kind:** related-source (Meluso; Matthew literature) — convergence across two isolated readings raises **salience, not statistical confidence** (per §0/§2 skeptic).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** Is verification/maintenance/care a first-class rewarded contribution in ResonantDAO accounting, or an unpaid externality? AND (2nd-order) does receipt observation actually *capture* invisible care work, or does the receipt boundary (ER-R10) systematically miss it the way "open" framing masks the OSS gap?
- **Tension pointer:** **see TF-9** — the pay-maintenance requirement is in *unresolved* tension with intrinsic-motivation crowd-out. TF-4 read alone must NOT be taken as a clean "pay everything" mandate.
- **Pressure point:** ER-R10, ER-R1, ER-R9.
- **Lanes:** Lane 3, Lane 2, Lane 5.
- **Verdict:** **requirement-residue (RES-level) — borrow-carefully on the requirement; future-work on every mechanism; routes to `authority/promotion-policy.md`, NOT promoted here.** (Skeptic hard REVISE: the prior `promotion-candidate` breached the §0 contract ceiling and laundered an open requirement into an advancing claim. Corrected.)
- **Per-finding collapse-test:** if a ResonantDAO receipt graph is ever observed to capture and reward maintenance/care work at parity with visible output, TF-4's requirement is *satisfied*, not open.

### TF-5 — Gatekeepers who both evaluate and benefit create a coercion channel — REVISED (magnitude quarantined)

- **Statement (structural):** A role that evaluates a contribution AND benefits from the credit metric is a coercion channel; reviewer-credit must be structurally separated from reviewed-credit. ResonantDAO curation/authority roles that can both gate credit and accrue it *may* inherit this **structure** — **magnitude untransferable**. The academic magnitudes are quarantined as antecedent: reviewer self-citation approval at OR=3.5 is a property of a 37k-review eLife dataset (Barnett 2025), and the "1-in-5 coerced" figure is **second-hand even in academia** (Wilhite–Fong primary is paywalled, RES-SoS-2). Neither number sizes a DAO effect.
- **Source-kind:** related-source (Barnett, structural) + secondary-reported (the 1-in-5 figure, weaker than related-source) + analogy (DAO role mapping).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** Do editors/reviewers map to ResonantDAO authority/curation roles, and if so is reviewer-credit structurally separated from reviewed-credit? Maps `attention-route-not-authority` / `owner-route-authority-only`.
- **Pressure point:** ER-R4, ER-R9, ER-R2.
- **Lanes:** Lane 1, Lane 2.
- **Verdict:** **borrow-carefully** on the separation requirement only; the academic magnitudes stay quarantined as citations, never as inherited DAO sizes.

### TF-6 — Collusion/rings must be witnessed BEFORE emission, not detected after — SURVIVES

- **Statement:** A reward system that emits on a credit signal and audits collusion afterward pays the ring before catching it. In the *academic citation* literature, rings are detectable only post-hoc and statistically (anomaly detection, PMC10836843). Whether on-chain mutual-endorsement/fork rings are *also* only post-hoc-detectable is an **open question, not a borrowed fact** — on-chain graphs may be observable pre-emission, which is the whole hope of this finding.
- **Source-kind:** related-source (anomaly-detection, *academic citation* scope) + local-inference (the "emit-then-detect is too late" sequencing).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** How do inter-author collusion patterns map to on-chain mutual-endorsement / voting / fork rings, and can an anti-collusion witness gate emission rather than follow it? Maps `credit-gaming-failure-fixtures`.
- **Pressure point:** ER-R2, ER-R1, ER-R4.
- **Lanes:** Lane 1, Lane 4, Lane 3.
- **Verdict:** **borrow-carefully** as a sequencing requirement (witness-before-emission); **block** "detect rings later" framing. Do not import "post-hoc-only" as an on-chain fact.

### TF-7 — Fairness witness must test distribution, not aggregate gaming rate — SURVIVES

- **Statement:** Extraction/coercion pressure concentrates on younger, non-native, and small actors (F5); Matthew dynamics compound for the already-eminent (D). An aggregate "gaming rate looks low" metric can hide that harm lands on the weakest tier. A fairness witness must test distributional impact across contributor power tiers. (Methodological constraint on *how you measure* — ports with least baggage; RI2's 965%/hyper-prolific signature stays out as a DAO figure, and the targeting-asymmetry claim is held at related-source-partial per RES-SoS-3.)
- **Source-kind:** related-source-partial (RI2 preprint) + analogy (DAO power tiers).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** What are ResonantDAO contributor power tiers, and does the anti-gaming/fairness witness test per-tier distributional impact rather than aggregate rate?
- **Pressure point:** ER-R9, ER-R2, ER-R8; ER-R5 if randomized audit is used.
- **Lanes:** Lane 4, Lane 1, Lane 5.
- **Verdict:** **future-work** (fairness-witness design is downstream).

### TF-8 — Novelty-bias and paradigm lock-in penalize boundary-spanning/minority work — SURVIVES (analogy-only)

- **Statement:** A credit metric that is paradigm-internal (citation/reuse mass) penalizes paradigm-challenging work until late (E); novelty earns higher citations while replication is discouraged (Romero, snippet-only). A reputation economy keyed to current-consensus reuse can under-reward minority-report and boundary-spanning contributions. The proposed cure (a minority-report channel insulated from reuse-mass) is **itself a new payable metric** and re-imports ER-R3 speculation / ER-R5 randomness-legitimacy risk.
- **Source-kind:** related-source (E) + **open-residue** (Romero fetch-failed, RES-SoS-1 — replication-discouragement claim under-sourced).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** Is there a forward-looking / minority-report reward channel insulated from current reuse-mass WITHOUT becoming a gameable speculation lottery?
- **Pressure point:** ER-R3, ER-R2, ER-R5.
- **Lanes:** Lane 3, Lane 2.
- **Verdict:** **analogy-only** (held below borrow line until Romero or a substitute is fetched; cure is TRAP-B-adjacent).

### TF-9 — Intrinsic motivation is crowded out when contribution is metricized/paid (central tension #2) — REVISED

- **Statement:** Benkler–Nissenbaum: commons-based peer production is mobilized by intrinsic motivation and virtue — **a result that holds for a population of intrinsically-motivated commons participants.** *If* ResonantDAO contributors are that population (an **assumption, not evidence** — unproven for this DAO), then attaching pay/score to contribution risks converting intrinsic motivation into extrinsic optimization (the motivational analogue of Goodhart). The intrinsic-population premise is stated here as a premise, not a given.
- **Source-kind:** related-source (Benkler–Nissenbaum) + operator-reading (crowding-out as a ResonantDAO risk, conditional on the population premise).
- **Bridge decision (UNRESOLVED):** Are ResonantDAO contributors intrinsically-motivated commons participants or extrinsically driven earners — and does the reward design protect the former without starving them?
- **Central tension #2 (pay-vs-crowd-out, visible from BOTH sides):** TF-4 demands maintenance/care be *paid* or it stays an unpaid externality; TF-9 warns that *paying* contribution crowds out intrinsic motivation **for an intrinsic population**. These are a genuine unresolved dilemma and must be resolved together — they are not independently satisfiable by default. (Cross-referenced from TF-4 above.)
- **Pressure point:** ER-R1 (the "task-harvesting labor market" failure), ER-R10.
- **Lanes:** Lane 2, Lane 1.
- **Verdict:** **borrow-carefully** (motivational warning, conditional on a stated population premise; do NOT assert ResonantDAO contributors are commons-virtue actors).
