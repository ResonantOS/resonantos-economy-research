# Distill

Status: post-research operator model; auditor ACCEPT
Promotion scope: local-research-only

## One-Sentence Model

A supply number is not a monetary system: evaluate typed events, schedules,
holdings/access, obligation coverage, executable exit, service use, and typed
relations as separate reports—and render no overall health verdict.

## What To Borrow Carefully

- typed state/event accounting with a completeness witness;
- horizon-specific schedule comparison;
- distribution and concentration trajectories;
- obligation-matched spendable-capacity and runway analysis;
- market-depth, price-impact, and time-to-cash stress;
- utility comparisons that isolate subsidy and speculation; and
- explicitly typed feedback edges and counterfactuals; and
- per-object evidence statuses in an incomplete MC-R record.

## What To Keep Analogy-Only

- another token's collapse as a direct forecast for `$RES`;
- banking or corporate thresholds as DAO-safe limits;
- market-capitalization or pool-value figures as treasury or exit capacity;
- generic velocity, liquidity, or decentralization targets; and
- a controller model as proof of legitimacy or safety;
- a generic market-impact loop named as a Diamond–Dybvig run; and
- external component precedent as a local `$RES` witness.

## What To Block

- choosing a schedule before initial conditions and failure criteria exist;
- treating fixed supply as a flow certificate;
- valuing runway entirely in the treasury's own token under an unstressed price;
- equating liquidity with supply, price, volume, or pool headline value;
- calling subsidy, yield, or buyback dependence utility;
- treating missing inputs as mechanism failure or zero risk;
- one generic reflexivity experiment;
- hiding a terminal or unknown state inside a top-level verdict; and
- using any diagnostic as reward, reputation, governance, or authority.
