# Monetary Circulation And Liquidity Resilience Research Tower

Status: bounded research dispatch closed; final auditor ACCEPT
Promotion scope: local-research-only
Validation state: no monetary, treasury, or liquidity experiment has run

## Purpose

This tower studies a missing dedicated program perspective: how supply,
release schedules, distribution, treasury capacity, liquidity, utility, and
market feedback interact over time.

The subject is not entirely new to the repository. Existing findings already
name flow conservation, emission concentration, treasury runway, thin-pool
risk, and open-loop feedback. Those fragments are spread across Lane 3, Lane 5,
formal-mechanism findings, and failed-system precedents. This tower gives them a
single source-first research boundary without silently treating them as a
finished token model.

The accepted research replaced a seven-peer “health vector” with independent
object reports and typed relations. It keeps these questions separate:

1. what supply exists and where it is held;
2. how units enter or leave circulation over time;
3. how holdings and access are distributed;
4. what obligations the treasury can meet under stress;
5. how much exit or exchange a market can absorb;
6. whether utility creates use without extraction pressure; and
7. which typed feedback edges amplify shocks or require bounded controls.

## Research Question

Under what supply, distribution, treasury, demand, and market conditions could
`$RES` circulate without producing exhaustion, concentration, extraction
pressure, or reflexive collapse?

## What This Tower Does Not Choose

The tower does not select:

- a supply cap or minting rule;
- an emission, unlock, vesting, or burn schedule;
- a treasury allocation or reserve policy;
- an AMM, exchange, market maker, liquidity provider, or listing;
- a price, yield, redemption, peg, or buyback target;
- a financial launch date or Phase 2 readiness verdict; or
- a monetary metric that grants reward, reputation, governance, or authority.

Any such move remains blocked behind the repository promotion route and the
open Phase 2 gates.

## Research Dimensions

| Dimension | Bounded question |
| --- | --- |
| Supply state | Can treasury-held, circulating, locked, emitted, and removed units be reconciled without double counting? |
| Release schedule | How do bounded, decreasing, finite-horizon growth, unlock, and burn schedules change affordability and sell pressure? |
| Distribution | Which schedule and allocation conditions create concentration, exclusion, or an incumbent advantage over time? |
| Treasury resilience | What positions, obligations, operating outflows, execution paths, and timing assumptions determine conditional coverage? |
| Liquidity and exit capacity | How much exchange or exit can occur under declared price-impact and time-to-cash limits, and who supplies that capacity? |
| Utility and sinks | Which uses create non-speculative demand, and which merely recycle subsidies, yield, buybacks, or expected appreciation? |
| Typed feedback | Which evidenced object-to-object edges amplify shocks, and what must a proposed control actually bound? |

These are an explicitly incomplete research subset. `MC-R` stores separate
per-object reports and a typed relation graph; it has no top-level PASS, FAIL,
score, rank, or health verdict.
