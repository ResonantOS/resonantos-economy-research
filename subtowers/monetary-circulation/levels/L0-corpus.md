## Adjudicated External Cohorts

The proposed dispatch decides admission. Listing a cohort or query here does not
establish that its conclusions transfer to a DAO.

### Supply, issuance, and distribution systems

- monetary and token-supply accounting;
- issuance, reward, unlock, vesting, burn, and dilution schedules;
- finite-horizon versus long-horizon distribution effects; and
- concentration, early-holder advantage, and newcomer access.

### Treasury solvency and reserve management

- reserve composition and correlated-asset risk;
- obligations, burn, haircuts, and time-to-cash;
- treasury diversification and forced-selling precedents; and
- stress-testing methods that distinguish book value from spendable capacity.

### Liquidity and market microstructure

- AMM and order-book depth, slippage, and price impact;
- liquidity-provider withdrawal and concentration;
- manipulation, oracle, sandwich, bank-run, and exit-capacity failure; and
- differences between reported liquidity, executable liquidity, and stressed
  liquidation.

### Utility, demand, and behavioral feedback

- transactional utility, sinks, velocity, hoarding, and redemption pressure;
- speculative demand, subsidy dependence, mercenary liquidity, and yield
  chasing;
- expectation effects around emission or reward changes; and
- reflexive feedback between price, treasury capacity, participation, and
  sell pressure.

## Search Bound

- One depth-1 web pass per external lane.
- At most six admitted sources per lane.
- At least one mechanism-attacking or failure precedent per lane, or a
  documented bounded no-result.
- Prefer primary papers, official operating documentation, first-party
  postmortems, and auditable datasets.
- No recursive bibliography chase; cited-but-unfetched sources become residue.
- Every source records source kind, supported claim, unsupported inference,
  local bridge, and disanalogy.
