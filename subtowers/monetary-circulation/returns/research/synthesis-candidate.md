# Synthesis candidate — monetary circulation and liquidity resilience

**Synthesizer:** Beer, Stafford  
**Dispatch:** `2026-07-13-monetary-circulation-research`  
**Status:** Candidate picture for downstream skeptic attack; no experiment verdict  
**Authority posture:** Local research only. External sources remain related precedent; the ResonantDAO whitepaper remains the only official primary source.

The four returns support a coupled state-and-feedback model, not a preferred token design:

```text
supply-state ledger
  → release/removal schedule
  → cohort/entity distribution
  → treasury obligations and stressed reserves
  → utility- or expectation-driven demand
  → executable market/exit capacity
  → adaptive price, participation, reserve, and liquidity feedback
```

No axis proves another. Lane 3 demotions remain: TF-1 is a structural flow rule with missing inputs; TF-12 awaits a crossover horizon `T*`; TF-14 awaits a divergence metric and bound `C`. Lane 5 remains Phase-2-gated analogy/stress precedent; TF-13 contributes only its zero-buffer inequality, not its old numeric thresholds. ER-D24–ER-D27 remain candidates with their grounding gaps.

## 2. Seven-dimension synthesis

| Dimension | Candidate synthesis and preserved conflict | Decision boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Supply state** | A headline total cannot reconcile minted, treasury-held, locked, vested, claimable, circulating, fee-transferred, rewarded, and removed units. Demand-sensitive burn makes net supply path-dependent. [Agent 1/Myerson, “EIP-1559”; “Introducing UNI”; local formal-TF-1] | **Borrow carefully.** MC-D1 remains candidate; source-backed Resonant initial conditions and mutually exclusive/nested state rules are absent. `$RCT` remains disjoint from `$RES`. |
| **2. Release schedule** | No schedule is uniformly safe. Filecoin attacks pure front-loaded exponential release under activity-growth assumptions; Fanti finds geometric rewards maximally equitable inside an honest proportional-stake class; formal-TF-12 finds geometric/growth-shaped rewards centralizing in a different long-run process; Budish shows finite issuance can strand a recurring obligation. EIP-1559 adds demand-conditioned removal. [Agent 1/Myerson, Filecoin; Fanti; Budish; EIP-1559; formal-TF-12] | **Split by reward basis, compounding rule, recipient process, obligation, and horizon.** Finite-horizon advantage and long-run concentration are different verdict fields. `T*`, epoch, affordability, and terminal funding remain parameters/blockers. |
| **3. Distribution trajectory** | Announced cohort allocation is not an entity-level outcome. Current-balance compounding may concentrate even honestly, early access may be structurally privileged, and address data cannot resolve people. Reward per useful unit, newcomer acquisition route, entry epoch, and redistribution must coexist in the vector. [Agent 1/Myerson, UNI; Filecoin; Fanti; Kondor] | **Borrow carefully; no threshold.** Cohorts, entity-resolution rule, horizon, compounding, participation/exit process, and access-failure predicate are missing. No distribution measure becomes merit or authority. |
| **4. Treasury resilience** | Solvency depends on obligation-matched stressed capacity, not ticker count or native-token book value. LFG rapidly consumed BTC/stables against peg defense; IRON’s endogenous reserve and lagged controller reinforced a run; Gitcoin’s nominal multi-year runway collapsed across price scenarios and sale discounts; CoW distinguishes defense, investable, and deployed positions. [Agent 2/Taleb, LFG; IRON/TITAN; Gitcoin; CoW; formal-TF-13] | **Retain narrow zero-buffer witness; split broader stress test.** ER-D24 remains role-known/value-open. ER-D27 thresholds remain parameters. Reserve state, obligations, encumbrance, correlated shocks, haircut function, and time-to-cash are ungrounded locally. |
| **5. Liquidity/exit capacity** | Liquidity is an executable path indexed by size, venue, range/book state, provider availability, fees, inclusion latency, and recovery. TVL and volume can remain high while active depth disappears; temporary capital can manipulate connected routes; Maker’s nominal collateral became poorly executable when congestion and bidder withdrawal coincided. [Agent 3/Fritz, Uniswap v3; Cont et al.; Qin et al.; Maker; Zhu et al.] | **Build from owned, locally blocked.** MC-D4 is sound only with a market structure, provider set, route graph, shock, and declared impact/time parameters. There is no admitted `$RES` venue, LP, oracle, or redemption right. |
| **6. Utility/sink integrity** | Token use can coordinate trade under narrow beliefs, but price-led entry, subsidy retention, yield migration, or public reward counts do not prove service utility. Incentives may crowd out visible prosocial meaning or increase participation while relocating it rather than creating net contribution. [Agent 4/Bowles, Li and Mann; Catalini/Tucker; Auer et al.; Ariely et al.; Lacetera et al.] | **Revise into isolation plus displacement tests.** MC-D5 needs a service-use event, comparison arms, horizon, quality/persistence measure, net-new versus shifted activity, and extraction measure. Direct sink, velocity, hoarding, and buyback-cessation evidence remains absent. |
| **7. Feedback/control** | Operated and formal sources support several distinct loops, not one generic “reflexivity” claim. Controller latency, provider withdrawal, treasury sales, reward compounding, price-attention entry, norm shifts, and yield migration require different state variables and bounds. [Agent 2/Taleb, IRON/TITAN and LFG; Agent 3/Fritz, Maker and Diamond–Dybvig; Agent 4/Bowles, Auer, Ariely, Lacetera, Aramonte; formal-TF-14] | **Split/future-work.** MC-D6 requires signed edges, delay, shock envelope, and divergence predicate. TF-14 remains `AWAITS` a metric and `C`; no governor or Phase-2 mechanism is selected. |

**Conflict rule:** a schedule result cannot travel without five labels: reward basis, compounding/reinvestment process, recipient/selection process, evaluation horizon, and participation/entry/exit assumptions. Filecoin’s activity-conditioned anti-front-loading rationale, Fanti’s proportional-stake geometric result, and TF-12’s long-horizon centralization claim concern different systems; flattening them into “geometric good/bad” would be false synthesis.

## 3. Explicit candidate feedback loops

1. **Compounding concentration:** current holdings → proportional reward probability/amount → larger holdings → larger future reward. It weakens if rewards are non-proportional, non-compounding, redistributed, or dominated by entry/exit. [Agent 1/Myerson, Fanti; formal-TF-12]

2. **Native-treasury forced sale:** native price decline → lower spendable treasury capacity → more native units sold per obligation → market impact → further price/capacity decline. Gitcoin is a scenario witness; it is not a calibrated Resonant loop. [Agent 2/Taleb, Gitcoin]

3. **Endogenous collateral/run:** endogenous reserve price falls faster than valuation/controller update → underpriced mint or advantaged redemption → liability sale/endogenous issuance → further reserve-price decline and external-reserve depletion. [Agent 2/Taleb, IRON/TITAN]

4. **Exit-depth run:** expected exit → cancellation/withdrawal/early sale → executable depth depletion and worse clearing → stronger expected exit. [Agent 3/Fritz, Cont et al.; Maker; Diamond–Dybvig]

5. **Concentrated-liquidity discontinuity:** price crosses active ranges → positions become inactive/one-sided → realized impact rises → LP rebalancing or withdrawal → less depth. [Agent 3/Fritz, Uniswap v3; Zhu et al.]

6. **Price-attention loop:** price rise → retail attention/entry → demand exposure → price sensitivity; reversal can produce losses and exit rather than service persistence. [Agent 4/Bowles, Auer et al.]

7. **Reward-norm/displacement loop:** visible payment → changed inferred motive or migration toward paid venue/epoch → apparent local growth → uncompensated activity or adjacent venue depleted. The sign is population- and visibility-dependent: Ariely supplies crowd-out evidence; Lacetera supplies positive participation plus displacement. [Agent 4/Bowles, Ariely; Lacetera]

8. **Open-loop affordability:** fixed yield/emission target → obligation exceeds sustainable inflow/reserve → selling or additional emission → price/treasury impairment → larger unaffordability gap. This remains a template until `sustainable_rate`, delay, shock envelope, metric, and `C` exist. [formal-TF-14; Agent 1/Myerson, Budish; Agent 4/Bowles, Aramonte]

## 4. Non-compensatory diagnostic vector

Report:

```text
MC-V(scenario, horizon) =
  <A_accounting, S_schedule, D_distribution,
   T_treasury, L_exit, U_utility, F_feedback>
```

Each component returns `{evidence_state, observed_vector, assumptions, terminal_failure}`; there is no weight, sum, rank, or overall “health” result.

| Component | Minimum observed vector | Terminal condition remains visible |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `A` | unit reconciliation by state and epoch; unexplained residual; `$RCT/$RES` separation | omitted/double-counted units, or `$RCT` summed into `$RES` |
| `S` | gross issue, removal, unlock, obligation, successor funding, horizon | declared obligation unaffordable or terminal continuation unspecified |
| `D` | entity/cohort balances, access route, entry epoch, reward basis, compounding | declared access/concentration failure parameter crossed |
| `T` | unencumbered/deployed/defense reserves, obligations, haircut-by-size/time/scenario | stressed spendable numerator non-positive while burn is positive |
| `L` | order size, route, active depth, providers, impact, inclusion/recovery time | frozen impact/time bound missed or required providers unavailable |
| `U` | service events, quality, persistence, subsidy/appreciation/yield/buyback arms, displacement | activity cannot be distinguished from isolated extraction driver |
| `F` | signed loop edges, delays, loop-specific state, shock envelope, controller action | any declared divergence predicate exceeded |

A PASS on `L` cannot repair `T`; `T` cannot repair `D`; price cannot repair `U`; and no weighted average can hide any terminal flag. [MC-D7; `TOWER.md` Measurement Contract; candidate MC-C7]

## 7. Typed-negative candidates

| Attractive candidate killed | What it would have contributed | Exact zeroing fact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **“Fixed/finite stock establishes sustainable flow.”** | simple monetary sufficiency claim | EIP-1559 makes net stock demand-path-dependent; Budish shows recurring obligations remain after bounded issuance; formal-TF-1 says the flow certificate is absent. |
| **“One reward curve is equitable across horizons.”** | schedule selection shortcut | Fanti’s geometric result depends on proportional-stake honest compounding, while Filecoin attacks front-loading under activity/value timing and formal-TF-12 reverses at long horizon. |
| **“Treasury quantity × spot price is runway.”** | easy solvency estimate | Gitcoin’s runway changes sharply under price, burn, discount, and liquidation assumptions; LFG’s obligation consumed executable reserves despite multiple asset labels. |
| **“Diversified ticker count proves reserve resilience.”** | simple reserve-quality claim | LFG’s assets did not independently cover the peg obligation, and CoW had to exit/reallocate positions after shared venue/liquidity incidents. |
| **“TVL, volume, or market capitalization is exit capacity.”** | cheap liquidity proxy | Uniswap range state, Cont’s depth/imbalance evidence, Zhu’s TVL/concentration split, and Maker bidder withdrawal make execution state- and size-dependent. |
| **“Price/app entry proves utility.”** | observable adoption proxy | Auer observes price-led exchange entry, not service transactions; Li and Mann’s coordination result requires assumptions that holding/resale alone cannot satisfy. |
| **“Incentives only help” / “incentives only harm.”** | universal behavior rule | Ariely’s sign depends on visibility/image meaning; Lacetera finds increased attendance plus displacement; Catalini shows access is mediated by social sequence. |
| **“One monetary-health score can trade strengths against failures.”** | ranked decision surface | the objects have different owners and terminal predicates; compensation would hide insolvency, illiquidity, exclusion, or absent utility and violate MC-D7/non-collapse. |

## 8. Definition and evidence-harness handoffs

### Definition handoff

Preserve, do not promote:

- **ER-D24 Treasury:** role only; add explicit open fields for reserve class, encumbrance, obligation served, and stressed executable value. Size remains unverified.
- **ER-D25 Epoch:** retain as fixture parameter; require sensitivity across candidate bases because affordability can change with period choice.
- **ER-D26 Emission:** retain as flow, separate gross issuance from unlock, transfer, burn/removal, and post-subsidy obligation.
- **ER-D27 Runway:** retain only zero-buffer inequality. Replace any implicit constant haircut with a candidate function `haircut(asset, size, venue, time, scenario)`; all numbers remain parameters.
- **MC-D1–D7:** retain as tower-local candidates. Add tracked candidate primitives for `reward_basis`, `compounding_rule`, `recipient_process`, `cohort/entity`, `obligation_due`, `reserve_encumbrance`, `provider_availability`, `service_use_event`, `displacement`, `shock_envelope`, and `divergence_condition`.

### `research-evidence-harness` handoff

Every fixture schema should require:

1. source posture and frozen provenance;
2. initial state and unit/state partition;
3. schedule/action plus reward basis, recipients, compounding, and horizons;
4. obligations, reserve classes, encumbrances, haircut/time-to-cash functions;
5. market route/provider graph and executable order sizes;
6. utility comparison arms and displacement accounting;
7. signed feedback edges, delays, shock envelope, and controller action;
8. the seven-component vector with independent invalidity predicates;
9. all numeric bounds marked `source-backed`, `calibrated`, or `scenario-parameter`;
10. explicit non-effects: no policy, payout, authority, governance, launch, or validation result.

The harness may validate JSON/JSONL shape, conservation calculations, scenario completeness, and metric computation. It must not manufacture missing `$RES` initial conditions or run live monetary experiments under this dispatch.

## 9. Residue and honest cutoff

Still open: official `$RES` quantity and state ledger; actual emission/unlock/burn schedule; recipient and participation process; reward compounding; entity/cohort mapping; treasury composition and obligations; external market, LPs, market makers, routes, oracle, redemption rights, and legal access; empirical utility/sink/velocity/hoarding/buyback evidence; calibrated shock, concentration, slippage, drawdown, depeg, time-to-cash, and divergence parameters; and any legitimate governor.

Explorer-cited but unfetched material remains residue, including current Ethereum issuance, UNI contracts/entity concentration, Filecoin linked allocation pages, direct LP-withdrawal and MEV datasets, Gitcoin detailed reports, CoW Dune/incident records, Maker’s complete postmortem, buyback/sink cessation studies, and longitudinal utility/reward-change datasets.

**Honest cutoff:** the new corpus prevents the tower’s collapse-test from reducing it to cross-links alone: it adds schedule-basis reversals, obligation-matched reserve evidence, executable-liquidity quantities, utility-isolation conflicts, and named feedback loops not jointly owned by the prior Lane 3/Lane 5 synthesis. That establishes a useful research owner and a sharper fixture backlog—not a safe economy, a validated mechanism, Phase-2 readiness, or a ResonantOS policy.
