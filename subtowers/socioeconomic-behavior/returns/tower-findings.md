## A. Convergence Map (cross-witness, before per-finding records)

**Strong convergence — EXISTENCE/DIRECTION of crowding-out (3 independent methods).**
All three lanes reach the same *direction* from non-overlapping evidence: attaching
explicit reward to a previously intrinsic/relational act substitutes external for
internal motivation.

- Kahneman F4/F5 (cognitive): once a reward sets a reference point, removing or
  reframing it is processed as a *loss* and as illegitimate — the act is now priced.
- Bowles F1/F2/F5 (experimental): field/lab demonstration that price and control
  signals reduce voluntary cooperation, and that incentives selectively reshape which
  preferences survive (composition).
- Hirschman F1/F6 (exit dynamics): the same substitution observed as *behavior over
  time* — locus shifts external, uncompensated activity declines, mercenary inflow
  exits when the reward stops.

This three-lane convergence is the tower's highest-confidence result **for the
existence and direction of motive substitution only**. Confidence is in *direction*,
not magnitude (see §C external-validity flag).

**SINGLE-STUDY — PERSISTENCE / HYSTERESIS (one Haifa field study; NOT convergent).**
The separate claim that crowding-out *persists after the reward is removed* (A'<A)
rests on **only Bowles F1**, whose persistence evidence is the **single Haifa daycare
field study** (late pickups stayed high after the fine was removed) plus Boston Fire.
Kahneman witnesses reference-point loss *on cuts* (TF-2), not persistence-after-
removal; Hirschman witnesses *locus shift*, not A'<A. **The hysteresis sub-claim is
single-study, single-lane, borrow-carefully — it is NOT "3-lane STRONG."** Promoting
the Haifa result into a general behavioral law is exactly the daycare→token-emissions
leap the corpus cannot support.

**Moderate convergence — visibility/control as a double-edged signal (2 lanes).**
Bowles F2/F3 (incentive/monitoring reads as distrust; reputation becomes a status game
that collapses when unobserved) and Hirschman F3/F4 (opaque/subjective credit +
surveillance-driven review burnout) converge on: *making contribution legible for
reward simultaneously makes it a surveillance and status surface*. Kahneman F3/F5
supports the legitimacy half but does not witness the surveillance-burnout half — so
the *burnout* edge is single-lane (Hirschman), not convergent.

**Weak / single-witness — procedural-fairness primacy.**
"Process/explanation beats outcome" is carried mainly by Kahneman F5 and the
procedural>distributive social-psych source; Hirschman's effective-vs-affective voice
(F2) is an *analogy-grade* parallel from a different literature, not an independent
confirmation of the same construct. Borrow-carefully, not convergent proof.

**Tension / divergence to preserve.**
- Reciprocity is a *surplus*, not only a hazard (Bowles F4 / TF-7) — cuts against a
  naive "all incentives crowd out" reading. Must not be flattened.
- Visibility helps *and* harms (Bowles F3 / TF-5): observation *raises* contribution
  yet *collapses* to zero when unwatched and is gameable. Sign is context-dependent.

**M-2 guard (triangulation over-claim).** The three lanes are three *descriptions* of
motive substitution; they were never shown to be the *same* effect in the *same*
population. Convergence of *direction* is earned; convergence as *triangulated proof
of magnitude* is not. The word "STRONG" applies to direction-existence only and is
withheld from every magnitude or persistence claim.

## B. Tower Findings (per-finding records)

Source-kind labels: primary-source / related-source / local-inference / analogy /
operator-reading / open-residue. Lanes per `lanes/ATTACK-LANES.md`.
Numeric witness thresholds below are **test-design choices**, not predictions the

### TF-1 — Crowding-out / motive substitution: existence is convergent, persistence is single-study
> **KEYSTONE — inline collapse-test:** this finding zeroes out if, in a within-subject
> in-tower run, **A' ≥ A** (no persistence) **OR** the Haifa-style hysteresis result
> fails to replicate in any tokenized setting. Existence-direction would still survive
> a persistence collapse; the *persistence* sub-claim does not.

- **Statement.**
  - *Existence/direction (3-lane convergent):* attaching explicit reward or control to
    an act that was previously intrinsic/relational substitutes external for internal
    motivation. Witnessed independently by framing, by field+lab experiment, and by
    exit behavior.
  - *Persistence/hysteresis (single field study — borrow-carefully, NOT STRONG):* the
    effect *may persist after the reward is removed* (A'<A). This rests only on the
    Haifa daycare field study (+ Boston Fire), Bowles F1 — one direction, one study.
  - *Composition:* incentives may shift *who* participates toward the mercenary.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source for direction (Bowles 2008/2016 field cases; KKT
  1986 dual-entitlement) + related-source (Frey/Jegen motivation-crowding,
  overjustification); **persistence sub-claim = single primary field study (Haifa).**
- **ResonantDAO pressure point.** The premise that `$RES` emissions can attach to
  "everything" / formerly-volunteer acts; first-payout events on intrinsic work.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 3 (`$RES` emission), Lane 1 (credit), Lane 5 (Phase-2 yield
  reinforces locus shift).
- **Witness (falsifiable, two separate falsifications).** Within-subject A→B→A' on an
  unpaid contribution stream: introduce reward (B), remove it (A').
  1. *Crowding-out (A→B):* confirmed if contribution under reward differs from the
     intrinsic baseline in the substitution direction.
  2. *Persistence/hysteresis (A'<A):* confirmed only if A' < A. If A' ≥ A, the
     persistence claim fails and the finding weakens to transient crowding.
  Companion longitudinal witness: zero-reward contribution share across epochs vs
  control cohort — declining share = composition drift.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** for existence/direction (downgrade-proof
  vs single-study); **borrow-carefully + future-work** for persistence (single Haifa
  study, untested in token settings). NOT a promotion-candidate.

### TF-2 — Reference-point entitlement makes emission cuts read as exploitation
- **Statement.** Contributors entitle to the terms of the most recent transaction;
  cutting `$RES`/`$RCT` from an established level is judged unfair and felt as a loss
  (~2x weight), independent of the new level's objective fairness. Demand-driven raises
  of the bar are judged unfair; cost-shock-framed adjustments are tolerated.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (KKT 1986 — 83% unfair existing-worker cut vs 27%
  new-joiner; dual-entitlement; read verbatim).
- **Pressure point.** `$RES` emission cuts, `$RCT` recalibration, retroactive epoch
  adjustments, throttling under participation spikes.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 3, Lane 1, Lane 5.
- **Witness.** A/B vignette over an emission-cut event; **fairness gap > 40pp**
  (test-design threshold, not a prediction) between "cut existing" and "lower
  new-joiner" framings = governance hazard. Cost-frame vs demand-frame fairness delta
  on the same numeric change. Caveat: this is a vignette (espoused unfairness judgement)
  not theory-in-use exit behavior — external validity to crypto contributors untested.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** (strong verbatim primary; pair with a
  future-work in-tower vignette run).

### TF-3 — Legitimacy of randomized / opaque allocation is conditional, not free
- **Statement.** People dislike randomization and opaque selection even when it is
  "fair," especially when a higher-claim contributor loses; legitimacy requires
  arbitrary-but-stated criteria, proportional chances, and stable public rules.
  Opaque/subjective credit erodes loyalty base-wide and lowers the exit threshold.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (KKT/Synthese 2025 on fairness & randomness) +
  related-source (Hirschman loyalty/influence; Starke algorithmic-fairness perception).
- **Pressure point.** Bounty/prize *random* rewards; opaque `$RCT` selection; "human
  trust state" scoring with no published formula.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 4, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Three-arm test — deterministic threshold vs weighted lottery vs flat
  random — measure perceived legitimacy specifically *in the case where a high-claim
  contributor loses*. Separately: publish the `$RCT` derivation and measure
  dispute-rate per 100 credit events before/after publication (theory-in-use metric).
- **Contract decision.** **block** unpublished/opaque credit formulas and unstructured
  random rewards as currently specified (discharges the AGENTS.md randomness
  non-negotiable); **future-work** for a legitimacy-tested weighted-lottery design.

### TF-4 — Incentive/monitoring contracts can signal distrust and depress effort
- **Statement.** Explicit incentive contracts and tight monitoring crowd out
  reciprocity; "I'm watching you" can reduce effort *below* a no-control baseline.
  Slashing, conditional escrow, and tight bounty gates risk reading as distrust.
- **Source-kind.** **primary/related (mixed)** — direction is primary (Bowles
  control-aversion / gift-exchange line); the surprising **below-baseline arm rests on
  a study that was a 429 residue read via review = related-source for that arm.** Lead
  label deliberately mixed so the roll-up does not read cleaner than the corpus.
- **Pressure point.** Slashing, conditional escrow, tight bounty gates, fraud-control
  surveillance.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 4, Lane 1.
- **Witness.** Trust-frame vs monitored-contract at *equal pay*; effort/contribution
  delta (theory-in-use). Distrust effect confirmed if monitored < trust at equal pay.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully**; the below-baseline clause carries the
  weakest source — flag slashing/escrow framing as a design variable to test, not a
  settled "safe" control.

### TF-5 — Visible reputation converts stewardship into a gameable status game that collapses when unobserved
- **Statement (witnessed halves).** Observation raises contribution (competitive
  altruism, partner choice) — but post-selection contribution falls to ~zero and the
  surface is gameable. Net: visibility is a *conditional* lever with a real witnessed
  downside, not a pure good.
- **OPEN TENSION (demoted — NOT a stated property).** Whether visibility's sign
  *flips under financial/transferable-asset stakes* ("receivers discount the signal
  when stakes are highest") is **not witnessed**. Barclay-Willer stakes are lab
  partner-choice stakes, not financial stakes — this clause is the external-validity
  gap (§C) restated, not a result. Treated as a future-work hypothesis requiring a
  stake-level manipulation arm before it is falsifiable in-tower.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (Barclay & Willer 2007 — observed p=0.001, partner
  choice p=0.021, top contributor 17/18, post-selection median 0) for the witnessed
  halves; **the financial-stakes sign-flip = open-residue / future-work.**
- **Pressure point.** Leaderboards, visible `$RCT`/reputation, demotions.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2, Lane 4.
- **Witness.** Observed vs anonymized contribution; post-"win" decay curve. (The
  stakes-discount arm is not currently falsifiable in-tower — add a stakes-level arm or
  it stays a hypothesis.) Status-game hazard confirmed if observed ≫ anonymized AND
  post-win contribution decays to baseline-or-below.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** for the witnessed halves; **future-work /
  analogy-only** for the financial-stakes sign-flip. Tension vs TF-7 preserved.

### TF-6 — Loss aversion distorts contribution around decay/clawback/demotion
- **Statement.** Losses weigh ~2x gains; `$RCT` decay, clawbacks, rank-drops, and
  use-it-or-lose-it emission are felt as outsized losses → disengagement and safe-task
  hoarding (people protect held credit rather than take risk).
- **Source-kind.** primary-source for the behavioral direction (KKT) — the **~2x
  magnitude leans on the canonical figure = related-source** (loss-aversion encyclopedia
  source was a 403 partial, supporting-only), not this corpus.
- **Pressure point.** `$RCT` decay, leaderboard demotions, use-it-or-lose-it emission.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Pre/post contribution around decay events; loss/gain sensitivity ratio
  **> 1.5** (test-design threshold borrowed from the ~2x literature, NOT a prediction
  the tower has earned) AND a measurable shift toward safe tasks (behavioral
  theory-in-use witness).
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully**; decay schedules are a design risk to
  test, not assume neutral.

### TF-7 — Reciprocity is a recoverable SURPLUS under relational framing (counter-balance)
- **Statement.** Unconditional, relationally-framed gifts *raise* productivity,
  **strongest in long-term relationships**. The crowding-out story is therefore not a
  blanket ban on rewards — *how* a reward is framed (gift/recognition vs price/contract)
  flips its sign. Anonymous one-shot rewards forfeit this surplus.
- **Boundary (skeptic-added).** The surplus is *weakest exactly where a bootstrapping
  DAO lives* — new cohorts, short relationships. So the design implication ("frame as
  gift/recognition") is *least supported in the population the DAO actually onboards*.
  State this; do not let it imply easy gains for new contributors.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (Bellemare & Shearer, IZA 2007, fetched).
- **Pressure point.** Recognition comms vs transactional payout; new-cohort vs
  long-tenure reward design.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 3, Lane 5.
- **Witness.** Unconditional-grant productivity uplift, long-tenure vs new cohort;
  recognition-frame vs payment-frame on identical payout.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** as a *design constraint*; preserve the
  tension with TF-1. Must NOT be flattened into the crowding-out story.

### TF-8 — Exit-by-default when voice is affective, not effective
- **Statement.** *By analogy to Hirschman (Exit, Voice, and Loyalty — base text
  unread):* when the only channel is affective (venting) rather than effective
  (binding) voice — no appeal process, undefined thresholds, custodian-led bootstrap —
  contributors *exit* instead of repairing. Loyalty requires perceived meaningful
  influence. The "by analogy" prefix is load-bearing: the EVL mechanism prose must not
  be lifted out of context as a primary claim.
- **Source-kind.** **analogy** for the core EVL mechanism (Hirschman internal pages not
  fetched, research.md residue); related-source (Frey & Schneider 2009,
  effective/affective voice, fetched) carries the *measurable* part only.
- **Pressure point.** Custodian-led bootstrap, no appeal process, undefined credit
  thresholds.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Effective-voice ratio (binding vs expressive channels) vs 90-day
  retention per sector — rests on Frey & Schneider, survives independently of the EVL
  analogy.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** for the measurable witness;
  **analogy-only** for the underlying EVL claim until the primary text is read.

### TF-9 — Review/surveillance load drives burnout-exit; review labor is unrewarded
- **Statement.** Unbounded governance + fraud-control review raises job demandingness
  (a burnout driver); review/validation labor is itself uncompensated; high burnout
  predicts quitting. Note: the 73%/60% figures are *espoused* ("considered leaving"),
  not theory-in-use (actual exits).
- **Source-kind.** related-source (Heath OSS burnout 2025 — 73% burnout, 60% considered
  leaving; survey-grade self-report, OSS not DAO → external validity partial).
- **Pressure point.** AI-assisted + custodian review unbounded; no rotation/load cap;
  unrewarded validators.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 4, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Validation actions/reviewer/week + 3-item burnout survey;
  80th-percentile load → 90-day exit hazard (measures *actual* exits — stronger than the
  source's self-report); recognition gap for reviewers.
- **Contract decision.** **future-work** (single-lane, OSS-survey external validity);
  surface a load-cap/rotation design question now.

### TF-10 — Discourse texture as a leading indicator of turnover (HYPOTHESIS, not result)
- **Statement.** *Hypothesis with a named test, not an established result:* rising
  "respectfulness"-themed discussion may predict more leaving / fewer joining;
  "social-power" discussion may predict better outcomes — a possible early-warning
  signal for exit. **The Granger-leads claim is NOT established.**
- **Source-kind.** related-source, **abstract-only** (ICSE 2024 — full text behind
  cross-host redirect; claim ≤ abstract).
- **Pressure point.** `$REX-COM` onboarding/moderation; absence of any discourse-health
  metric.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** NLP-classify threads; test whether respectfulness-share Granger-leads
  next-quarter exit. (This witness *is the experiment that would establish the claim* —
  confirming the finding is currently a hypothesis, not a signal.)
- **Contract decision.** **future-work**. Never treat as a witnessed signal.

### TF-11 — Bounty/prize design crowds out creative/educational intrinsic work and invites mercenary inflow
- **Statement.** Bounties attach expected reward to intrinsic creative/Academy work;
  post-cycle uncompensated baseline *may fall below* pre-bounty, and mercenary inflow
  arrives for the bounty and exits when it stops. Recognition-framing vs payment-framing
  is the lever.
- **TF-1 DEPENDENCY (explicit).** This finding inherits its mechanism from TF-1, and the
  "falls *below* pre-bounty" clause is a *hysteresis-flavoured* claim. Since TF-1's
  persistence sub-claim is downgraded to single-study (Haifa), **TF-11's below-baseline
  claim inherits that weakness and must NOT read as stronger than its parent.**
- **Source-kind.** local-inference combining TF-1 direction (primary) with Hirschman F6
  / overjustification (related-source) applied to the bounty path; the bounty-specific
  baseline-drop is inference, not observed here (Hirschman F6 is itself a projection).
- **Pressure point.** Marketplace/Academy bounty paths; no framing-policy.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 4, Lane 3, Lane 5.
- **Witness.** Randomized bounty subset; compare post-bounty uncompensated output,
  treated vs control. Crowding confirmed if treated post-baseline < control.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** for the mechanism; **future-work** for the
  bounty-specific magnitude. Discharges the Lane-4 / AGENTS.md non-negotiable: bounties
  are NOT safe without gaming/optimization analysis.
