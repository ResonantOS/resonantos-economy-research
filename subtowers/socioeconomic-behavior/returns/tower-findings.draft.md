## A. Convergence Map (cross-witness, before per-finding records)

**Strong convergence — crowding-out / mercenary drift (3 independent methods).**
All three lanes reach the same hazard from non-overlapping evidence:

- Kahneman F4/F5 (cognitive): once a reward sets a reference point, removing or
  reframing it is processed as a *loss* and as illegitimate — the act is now
  priced in the participant's head.
- Bowles F1/F2/F5 (experimental): direct field/lab demonstration that price and
  control signals reduce voluntary cooperation, that the reduction *persists after
  removal* (Haifa hysteresis A'<A), and that incentives selectively reshape which
  preferences survive (composition).
- Hirschman F1/F6 (exit dynamics): the same substitution observed as *behavior over
  time* — locus of motivation shifts external, uncompensated activity declines,
  mercenary inflow exits when the reward stops.

This is the **highest-confidence finding of the tower**: a *mechanism* (crowding /
motive substitution) witnessed by framing, by experiment, and by exit, each a
distinct falsification path. Confidence is in the *direction and existence* of the
effect, not its magnitude inside a DAO (see §C external-validity flag).

**Moderate convergence — visibility/control as a double-edged signal (2 lanes).**
Bowles F2/F3 (incentive/monitoring reads as distrust; reputation becomes a status
game that collapses when unobserved) and Hirschman F3/F4 (opaque/subjective credit
+ surveillance-driven review burnout) converge on: *making contribution legible for
reward simultaneously makes it a surveillance and status surface*. Kahneman F3/F5
supports the legitimacy half (opacity/randomness reads as unfair) but does not
witness the surveillance-burnout half — so the *burnout* edge is largely
single-lane (Hirschman) and should not be over-claimed.

**Weak / single-witness — procedural-fairness primacy.**
"Process/explanation beats outcome" is carried mainly by Kahneman F5 and the
procedural>distributive social-psych source; Hirschman's effective-vs-affective
voice (F2) is an *analogy-grade* parallel from a different literature, not an
independent confirmation of the same construct. Treat as borrow-carefully, not
convergent proof.

**Tension / divergence to preserve.**
- Reciprocity is a *surplus*, not only a hazard. Bowles F4 (unconditional gift
  raises productivity, strongest in long relationships) cuts *against* a naive
  "all incentives crowd out" reading: gifts framed as relational, not contractual,
  *add* cooperation. The tower must not flatten this into the crowding-out story.
- Visibility helps *and* harms: Bowles F3 shows observation *raises* contribution
  (competitive altruism) yet *collapses* to zero when unwatched and is gameable.
  The sign of the visibility effect is context-dependent — a real tension, not noise.

## B. Tower Findings (per-finding records)

Source-kind labels: primary-source / related-source / local-inference / analogy /
operator-reading / open-residue. Lanes per `lanes/ATTACK-LANES.md`.

### TF-1 — Crowding-out / motive substitution is a real, persistent mechanism (CONVERGENT)
- **Statement.** Attaching explicit reward or control to an act that was previously
  intrinsic/relational substitutes external for internal motivation; the effect can
  *persist after the reward is removed* (hysteresis) and can shift the *composition*
  of who participates toward the mercenary. Witnessed independently by framing
  (loss/reference), by field+lab experiment, and by exit behavior.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (Bowles 2008/2016 field cases — Haifa daycare,
  Boston Fire; KKT 1986 dual-entitlement) reinforced by related-source
  (Frey/Jegen motivation-crowding, overjustification).
- **ResonantDAO pressure point.** The premise that `$RES` emissions can attach to
  "everything" / formerly-volunteer acts; first-payout events on intrinsic work.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 3 (`$RES` emission), Lane 1 (credit), Lane 5 (Phase-2 yield
  reinforces locus shift).
- **Witness (falsifiable).** Within-subject A→B→A' on an unpaid contribution stream:
  introduce reward (B), remove it (A'). Crowding-out **confirmed if A' < A**; the
  hysteresis claim *fails* (and finding weakens to transient) if A' ≥ A. Companion
  longitudinal witness: zero-reward contribution share across epochs vs control
  cohort — declining share = composition drift.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** for the existence/direction;
  **future-work** for any DAO-specific magnitude (see external-validity flag).
  NOT a promotion-candidate — the witness has not been run *here*.

### TF-2 — Reference-point entitlement makes emission cuts read as exploitation
- **Statement.** Contributors entitle to the terms of the most recent transaction;
  cutting `$RES`/`$RCT` from an established level is judged unfair and felt as a
  loss (~2x weight), independent of the new level's objective fairness. Demand-driven
  raises of the bar are judged unfair; cost-shock-framed adjustments are tolerated.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (KKT 1986 — 83% unfair existing-worker cut vs 27%
  new-joiner; dual-entitlement).
- **Pressure point.** `$RES` emission cuts, `$RCT` recalibration, retroactive epoch
  adjustments, throttling under participation spikes.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 3, Lane 1, Lane 5.
- **Witness.** A/B vignette over an emission-cut event; **fairness gap > 40pp**
  between "cut existing" and "lower new-joiner" framings = governance hazard.
  Cost-frame vs demand-frame fairness delta on the same numeric change.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** (strong primary source, but vignette
  external validity to crypto contributors is untested → pair with future-work run).

### TF-3 — Legitimacy of randomized / opaque allocation is conditional, not free
- **Statement.** People dislike randomization and opaque selection even when it is
  "fair," especially when a higher-claim contributor loses; legitimacy requires
  arbitrary-but-stated criteria, proportional chances, and stable public rules.
  Opaque/subjective credit erodes loyalty base-wide and lowers the exit threshold.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (KKT/Synthese 2025 on fairness & randomness) +
  related-source (Hirschman loyalty/influence; Starke algorithmic-fairness perception).
- **Pressure point.** Bounty/prize *random* rewards; opaque `$RCT` selection;
  "human trust state" scoring with no published formula.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 4, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Three-arm test — deterministic threshold vs weighted lottery vs flat
  random — measure perceived legitimacy specifically *in the case where a
  high-claim contributor loses*. Separately: publish the `$RCT` derivation and
  measure dispute-rate per 100 credit events before/after publication.
- **Contract decision.** **block** unpublished/opaque credit formulas and
  unstructured random rewards as currently specified; **future-work** for a
  legitimacy-tested weighted-lottery design.

### TF-4 — Incentive/monitoring contracts can signal distrust and depress effort
- **Statement.** Explicit incentive contracts and tight monitoring crowd out
  reciprocity; "I'm watching you" can reduce effort *below* a no-control baseline.
  Slashing, conditional escrow, and tight bounty gates risk reading as distrust.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (Bowles gift-exchange / control-aversion lab work,
  one underlying study a 429 residue → read via review = related-source for that arm).
- **Pressure point.** Slashing, conditional escrow, tight bounty gates, fraud-control
  surveillance.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 4, Lane 1.
- **Witness.** Trust-frame vs monitored-contract at *equal pay*; effort/contribution
  delta. Distrust effect confirmed if monitored < trust at equal pay.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully**; flag slashing/escrow framing as a
  design variable to test, not a settled "safe" control.

### TF-5 — Visible reputation converts stewardship into a gameable status game that collapses when unobserved
- **Statement.** Observation raises contribution (competitive altruism, partner
  choice) — but post-selection contribution falls to ~zero, receivers discount the
  signal exactly when stakes are highest, and the whole surface is gameable. Net:
  visibility is a *conditional* lever with a real downside, not a pure good.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (Barclay & Willer 2007 — observed p=0.001, partner
  choice p=0.021, top contributor chosen 17/18, post-selection median 0).
- **Pressure point.** Leaderboards, visible `$RCT`/reputation, demotions.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2, Lane 4.
- **Witness.** Observed vs anonymized contribution; post-"win" decay curve; receiver
  discounting at high stakes. Status-game hazard confirmed if observed≫anonymized
  AND post-win contribution decays to baseline-or-below.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** (well-witnessed but sign is
  context-dependent — see TF-2 tension); leaderboard demotions also feed TF-6 loss
  aversion.

### TF-6 — Loss aversion distorts contribution around decay/clawback/demotion
- **Statement.** Losses weigh ~2x gains; `$RCT` decay, clawbacks, rank-drops, and
  use-it-or-lose-it emission are felt as outsized losses → disengagement and
  safe-task hoarding (people protect held credit rather than take risk).
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (loss aversion; KKT) — note the loss-aversion
  encyclopedia source was a 403 partial (supporting-only) so the *magnitude* leans on
  the canonical ~2x figure, related-source.
- **Pressure point.** `$RCT` decay, leaderboard demotions, use-it-or-lose-it emission.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Pre/post contribution around decay events; loss/gain sensitivity ratio.
  Distortion confirmed if ratio > 1.5 and a measurable shift toward safe tasks.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully**; decay schedules are a design risk to
  test, not assume neutral.

### TF-7 — Reciprocity is a recoverable SURPLUS under relational framing (counter-balance)
- **Statement.** Unconditional, relationally-framed gifts *raise* productivity,
  strongest in long-term relationships. The crowding-out story is therefore not a
  blanket ban on rewards — *how* a reward is framed (gift/recognition vs
  price/contract) flips its sign. Anonymous one-shot rewards forfeit this surplus.
- **Source-kind.** primary-source (Bellemare & Shearer, IZA 2007).
- **Pressure point.** Recognition comms vs transactional payout; new-cohort vs
  long-tenure reward design.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 3, Lane 5.
- **Witness.** Unconditional-grant productivity uplift, long-tenure vs new cohort;
  recognition-frame vs payment-frame on identical payout.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** as a *design constraint* (frame rewards
  as recognition/relationship, not price). Direct counter-evidence to a naive
  TF-1 reading; preserve the tension.

### TF-8 — Exit-by-default when voice is affective, not effective
- **Statement.** When the only channel is affective (venting) rather than effective
  (binding) voice — no appeal process, undefined thresholds, custodian-led bootstrap
  — contributors *exit* instead of repairing. Loyalty requires perceived meaningful
  influence.
- **Source-kind.** related-source (Frey & Schneider 2009, effective/affective voice)
  built on **analogy** to Hirschman's exit-voice-loyalty frame (base text, internal
  pages not fetched → analogy-grade for the core EVL mechanism).
- **Pressure point.** Custodian-led bootstrap, no appeal process, undefined credit
  thresholds.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Effective-voice ratio (binding vs expressive channels) vs 90-day
  retention per sector.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully**, but flagged **analogy-only** for the
  underlying EVL claim until the primary text is read; the *measurable* voice-ratio
  witness can stand on the Frey & Schneider source.

### TF-9 — Review/surveillance load drives burnout-exit; review labor is unrewarded
- **Statement.** Unbounded governance + fraud-control review raises job
  demandingness (a burnout driver); review/validation labor is itself uncompensated;
  high burnout strongly predicts quitting.
- **Source-kind.** related-source (Heath OSS burnout 2025 — 73% burnout, 60%
  considered leaving; survey-grade, OSS not DAO → external validity partial).
- **Pressure point.** AI-assisted + custodian review unbounded; no rotation/load cap;
  unrewarded validators.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 4, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** Validation actions/reviewer/week + 3-item burnout survey;
  80th-percentile load → 90-day exit hazard; recognition gap for reviewers.
- **Contract decision.** **future-work** (single-lane, OSS-survey external validity);
  surface a load-cap/rotation design question now.

### TF-10 — Discourse texture is a leading indicator of turnover
- **Statement.** Rising "respectfulness"-themed discussion predicts more leaving /
  fewer joining; "social-power" discussion predicts better outcomes — i.e. community
  discourse contains an early-warning signal for exit before exit happens.
- **Source-kind.** related-source (ICSE 2024 turnover-from-value-discussions —
  abstract only, full text behind cross-host redirect → **claim ≤ abstract**).
- **Pressure point.** `$REX-COM` onboarding/moderation; absence of any
  discourse-health metric.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 1, Lane 2.
- **Witness.** NLP-classify threads; test whether respectfulness-share
  Granger-leads next-quarter exit.
- **Contract decision.** **future-work** (abstract-only evidence; promising but
  under-sourced). Do NOT treat the Granger claim as established.

### TF-11 — Bounty/prize design crowds out creative/educational intrinsic work and invites mercenary inflow
- **Statement.** Bounties attach expected reward to intrinsic creative/Academy work;
  post-cycle uncompensated baseline may fall *below* pre-bounty, and mercenary inflow
  arrives for the bounty and exits when it stops. Recognition-framing vs
  payment-framing is the lever.
- **Source-kind.** local-inference combining TF-1 crowding mechanism (primary) with
  Hirschman F6 / overjustification (related-source) applied to the bounty path; the
  *bounty-specific* baseline-drop is inference, not directly observed here.
- **Pressure point.** Marketplace/Academy bounty paths; no framing-policy.
- **Attack-lanes.** Lane 4, Lane 3, Lane 5.
- **Witness.** Randomized bounty subset; compare post-bounty uncompensated output,
  treated vs control. Crowding confirmed if treated post-baseline < control.
- **Contract decision.** **borrow-carefully** for the mechanism; **future-work** for
  the bounty-specific magnitude. Directly satisfies a Lane-4 refusal-standard
  requirement ("bounties as safe without gaming analysis" — they are not safe).
