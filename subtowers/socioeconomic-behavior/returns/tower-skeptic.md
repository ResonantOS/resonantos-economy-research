## Attack stance (Argyris)

The draft's chronic risk is the gap between **espoused theory** ("people will
crowd out / exit / read distrust") and **theory-in-use** (what the cited subjects
*actually did* in *their* setting). Every place the draft narrates a behavioral law
in the present tense ("contributors entitle to…", "visibility raises contribution")
it is reporting an espoused regularity. The theory-in-use evidence is always
*someone else's subjects in a non-token setting*. A witness is genuinely
falsifiable only if it is a future observation **in this tower's environment**, not
a restatement of the source result. I graded every witness on that test.

I uphold the preserved external-validity flag (§C): no source tested any of these
effects inside a tokenized DAO. Any finding claiming more than **direction-borrowing**
is over-reach by construction. The flag is correct and load-bearing; do not weaken it.

## Per-finding verdicts

### TF-1 — Crowding-out / motive substitution (CONVERGENT) → **REVISE** (downgrade convergence claim)
The mechanism survives as *direction*. The **convergence claim is over-stated** and
must be split. The draft says the *persistent* effect (hysteresis, A'<A) is
"witnessed independently by framing, by field+lab experiment, and by exit." It is
not. Check the source record:
- The **persistence/hysteresis** evidence is **only** Bowles F1, and Bowles F1's
  persistence rests on the **single Haifa daycare field study** (late pickups stayed
  high after the fine was removed) plus Boston Fire. One field study, one direction.
- Kahneman (framing) witnesses *reference-point loss on cuts* (TF-2), **not**
  persistence-after-removal. Hirschman (exit) witnesses *locus shift*, not A'<A.
So the three-lane convergence is real for **"reward attaches → motive substitutes"**
but the **hysteresis sub-claim is single-study, single-lane**. Argyris point: the
draft promotes Haifa's theory-in-use into a general behavioral law and then re-labels
it "3-lane STRONG." That is the daycare→token-emissions leap the brief warns against.

Witness (`A→B→A'`, confirmed if A'<A) is **measurable and genuinely falsifiable** —
good. But the witness as written conflates *crowding-out* (A→B drop) with
*hysteresis* (A'<A persistence); they are two falsifications and the draft's pass/fail
rule only tests the second. Keep the witness, separate the two confidence levels.

REQUIRED REVISION: in TF-1 statement and the §D roll-up, mark **existence/direction =
3-lane**, **persistence/hysteresis = single field study (Haifa), borrow-carefully,
do NOT call STRONG**. Lanes (3,1,5) correct.

### TF-5 — Visible reputation → gameable status game that collapses unobserved → **SURVIVES**, but **the sign-flip claim is DEMOTE-TO-TENSION** (not a witnessed downside)
Adjudicating the synthesizer's flag: **"receivers discount the signal exactly when
stakes are highest" / sign-flip under financial stakes.** The Barclay & Willer 2007
result IS real and verbatim-grade (observed p=0.001, partner choice p=0.021, top
contributor 17/18, post-selection median 0). So the **collapse-when-unobserved** and
**post-win-decay** halves SURVIVE — measurable, falsifiable, theory-in-use.
BUT the draft's phrase "receivers discount the signal exactly when stakes are highest"
is doing work the source does not license **for a token/financial-stakes setting**:
Barclay-Willer stakes are lab partner-choice stakes, not transferable-asset financial
stakes. The claim that visibility's sign *flips under financial stakes* is **not
witnessed** — it is exactly the external-validity gap (§C) restated as if it were a
result. Verdict: keep the two witnessed halves; **demote the "highest stakes →
discount" clause to an OPEN tension / future-work hypothesis**, not a stated property.
Witness (observed vs anonymized + post-win decay curve) is measurable; the
receiver-discounting-at-high-stakes arm is **not currently falsifiable in-tower**
because no stake-level manipulation is specified. Add a stakes-level arm or drop the
clause. Lanes 1,2,4 correct. Tension vs TF-7 correctly preserved.

## Findings the synthesizer's flag set MISSED

**M-1 (most important miss). TF-1 convergence inflation — "3-lane STRONG" for a
property only one lane (and one field study) witnesses.** The synthesizer flagged
TF-1's *hysteresis source* but still left the headline calling the **whole** finding
"highest-confidence … 3-lane STRONG" in §A and §D. The inflation is not the source
note; it is the **strength label on the composite**. Splitting existence (3-lane) from
persistence (single Haifa study) is the single highest-value correction in this pass.

**M-2. §A "convergence" double-counts one mechanism as three witnesses.** The Senge
reconciliation stance treats the three lanes as "independent witnesses" of one loop.
Argyris read: framing-loss, lab crowding, and exit-behavior are three *descriptions*
of motive substitution, but they were never shown to be the *same* effect in the
*same* population — convergence of *direction* is real, convergence as *triangulated
proof of magnitude* is asserted, not earned. The draft mostly guards this ("confidence
is in direction, not magnitude") but the word "STRONG" in the table re-imports the
over-claim. Tie this fix to M-1.

**M-3. TF-5 financial-stakes clause and TF-2/TF-6 magnitude thresholds quietly assume
the external-validity flag away.** §C says sign and magnitude inside a token economy
are OPEN, yet TF-5 states a sign-condition ("discount when stakes highest") and
TF-6/TF-2 carry numeric pass-thresholds (>1.5, >40pp) that read as predictions. These
are fine **as witness thresholds** but must be labelled as test-design choices, not
borrowed results — otherwise they contradict the flag they sit under.

**M-4. No finding states its own collapse-test inline.** AGENTS.md requires any
keystone claim to carry its collapse-test bound to the headline. §A names TF-1 "the
highest-confidence finding of the tower" — that is a keystone claim and it does **not**
carry an inline collapse-test. Required by the non-negotiable: add to the TF-1 headline
the one fact that zeroes it — e.g. "collapse-test: A'≥A in the within-subject run, OR
the daycare result fails to replicate in any tokenized setting."
