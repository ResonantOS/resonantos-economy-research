# Short Initiative Video Transport Contract

## Identity

- Transport ID: `short_initiative_video_model_input_package`
- Proof status: candidate
- Target runtime: 60 seconds
- Voice target: 115–135 words at approximately 130 words per minute
- Primary frame: 16:9 for the site
- Crop contract: all load-bearing action and editorial copy remain inside a centered 9:16-safe area
- Target public: people who know nothing about ResonantDAO, the time capsule, or cryptography
- Objective: make a viewer feel invited and accurately understand that what people leave joins one shared capsule that helps choose how many tokens ResonantDAO starts with
- Desired residue: “I can leave something for later; what people leave becomes one shared capsule and helps form the starting token number.”

## Voice Rules

- Write for breath, not silent reading.
- Begin with a human reason to care before explaining the mechanism.
- Use “message parcel” only as a visual metaphor; the participant may leave a message,
  another small mark, or nothing.
- Say what the number is: how many tokens ResonantDAO starts with.
- Never imply that one message maps to a fixed number or grants tokens, status, or influence.
- Keep `42` visual-only. Pair it with the spoken correction “The real one comes later.”
- End with an invitation, not a command.

## Visual Rules

- Preserve the visual concept of the existing 1672:941 hero image.
- A parcel is a folded point of light with no readable message content.
- Connections appear because distinct paths meet; avoid generic globe networks.
- The capsule is one shared circular object, not one capsule per participant.
- The fingerprint forms only after the parcels join the capsule.
- A single public closing pulse meets the fingerprint before the number appears.
- Generate no exact typography. The editor creates `42`, time labels, and CTA copy.
- Avoid coins, token stacks, price charts, wallets, exchange imagery, blockchain logos,
  triumphant wealth imagery, cyberpunk neon, excessive particles, or surveillance maps.

## Honesty Boundaries

1. `42` is an illustrative easter egg, not the selected number.
2. A contribution changes the shared capsule; it is not given a personal token score.
3. The closing signal does not store message content.
4. The starting number is chosen once; later openings reveal messages, not new numbers.
5. The current site remains a design rehearsal and does not collect or protect messages.

## Completion Checks

The package is production-ready only when:

1. The operator approves the opening, tension, and reveal voice audition.
2. The generated voice is reviewed for natural emphasis and timing.
3. Representative clips prove parcel, capsule, fingerprint, and number continuity.
4. `42` is unmistakably an easter egg rather than a factual reveal.
5. The site version has captions, transcript, poster, play control, and a non-coercive CTA.
6. A first-time viewer can retell messages → capsule → fingerprint/closing signal → starting token number.

Audience learning and emotional impact require people; they cannot be proven by package validation.
