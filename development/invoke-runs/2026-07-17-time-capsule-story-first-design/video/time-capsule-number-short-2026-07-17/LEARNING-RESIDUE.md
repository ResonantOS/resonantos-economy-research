# Learning Residue

1. The visual anchor must persist through the whole short. A parcel introduced only in
   the opening would become decoration; carrying it through connection, capsule, and
   future return makes it explanatory.
2. “Packages” can look logistical or digital. “Message parcels of light” preserves the
   user’s image while avoiding delivery-company and data-packet associations.
3. The strongest impact point is not the number alone. It is the instant when many
   distinct paths become one shared fingerprint and then resolve into a number.
4. `42` works better as a visual-only easter egg. Naming the number and then explaining
   the joke makes it feel authored rather than discovered. The correction still belongs
   in the same beat: “The real one comes later.”
5. The number reveal needs stillness. Constant particle motion would make the moment feel
   like a casino or product launch.
6. A site video should deepen the plain-language explanation, never replace it. Captions,
   transcript, poster, and a user-started play action are part of the content contract.
7. The eight visual prompts remain unproven until representative clips preserve the same
   parcel, capsule, fingerprint, and restrained visual grammar across generation.
