# Learning Residue

1. The previous experience explained the system before giving the visitor a reason to care.
2. “Short message” is warmer than “phrase,” but the final product term still needs operator choice.
3. A single master fingerprint does not prevent staged release when saved messages have
   individual commitments inside the manifest.
4. Bitcoin can close and timestamp the ritual without carrying message content.
5. The story must not become a substitute for safety: recovery and saved-message custody remain open.
6. The strongest falsifier is a newcomer saying either “my phrase becomes my number” or
   “the messages are stored on Bitcoin.”
7. “Community time capsule” describes the shared container, not the required audience of
   every message. A participant may write to their future self, another person, or everyone.
8. Time prompts should state the actual horizon. “Two years from now” carries more useful
   meaning than poetic phrases such as “while this moment is still close.”
9. The artwork loses its role when placed in a framed card next to the hero. The opening
   works better when the image covers the section and the header lives inside it.
10. Full-image does not mean full-height at every viewport. On tall desktop screens, the
    section should follow the artwork's native 1672:941 proportion so the visual field is
    not stretched or excessively cropped.
11. A personal time-capsule invitation is not enough by itself. The first section must also
    name the shared consequence: the capsule helps choose how many tokens ResonantDAO
    starts with.
