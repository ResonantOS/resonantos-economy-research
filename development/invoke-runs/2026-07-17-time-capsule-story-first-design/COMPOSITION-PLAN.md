# Composition Plan

## Story Arc

1. **Invitation** — “Leave something for later.”
2. **Time opens** — the participant sees the three returns before seeing fields.
3. **Offering** — they write one short message or choose quiet.
4. **Agency** — they decide whether it is shared now or saved for its opening.
5. **Belonging** — their message becomes one record among many.
6. **Transformation** — records become a fingerprint; the closing block gives the ritual its number.
7. **Return** — at 2, 5, and 10 years, a person, another recipient, or the community meets the messages again.
8. **Honest closure** — the rehearsal receipt says nothing was sent or saved.

## Surface Map

| Beat | Surface | Text form |
| --- | --- | --- |
| invitation | hero | one question, one sentence, one CTA |
| time opens | timeline | three one-line future scenes |
| offering | form panel | short prompts and textareas |
| agency | choice panel | two plain choices |
| transformation | ritual panel | short labels + SVG path |
| closure | receipt panel | summary + boundary stamp |

## Candidate Set

| Candidate | Strength | Weakness | Result |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mechanism first | causal clarity | asks for attention before meaning | reject as lead; keep after action |
| Nostalgia first | emotional pull | can hide the number mechanism | retain with causal bridge |
| Rule cards | scannable | feels normative and heavy | reject |
| First-person future story | memorable, humane, compact | can become vague | select with concrete interaction |

## Pareto Selection

Selected: personally open future story + visible causal bridge. It preserves emotional pull,
causal accuracy, compactness, and voluntary tone without making the user study the full
ceremony before participating.
