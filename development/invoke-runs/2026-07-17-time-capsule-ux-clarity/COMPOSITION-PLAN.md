# Whisper Composition Plan

Status: `pass`

## Candidate Set

### A — Memory First (`selected`)

Open with the time capsule and a future-facing question. Explain the token
number as the capsule's first act, then show the three later returns.

Strength: immediately names the object and gives the number an emotional reason
to exist.

Risk: the number can feel secondary unless the lead names it plainly.

### B — Number First (`rejected`)

Open with the arbitrary starting supply problem and present words as the
solution.

Strength: direct connection to token supply.

Risk: resembles a contest, invites “how much is my word worth?”, and repeats
the prior comprehension failure.

### C — Ritual First (`rejected`)

Open with collective declaration and founding symbolism.

Strength: strongest manifesto resonance.

Risk: asks the reader to feel significance before they understand the action.

## Selected Smallest Coherent Unit

> Leave words. Shape one beginning. Meet them again.

This unit recomposes into the complete story:

- **Leave words** → up to two words at 2, 5, and 10 years; quiet is valid.
- **Shape one beginning** → the whole capsule plus a later public input selects
  one initial supply inside a pre-agreed range.
- **Meet them again** → words return at future openings; the number stays fixed.

## Proposer / Balancer — Round 1

### Proposer

Use a question-led hero followed by one visual equation:

```text
our words -> one shared pattern -> one starting number
same words -> 2y / 5y / 10y returns
```

### Balancer

“A number and three openings are still two concepts. If the lead compresses
them into one sentence, the reader may remember only the poetic half.”

### Reconciliation

Use a direct lead, then a separate correction line:

> Together, our words help choose the number of tokens ResonantDAO begins
> with—and return to us in 2, 5, and 10 years.

> The number is chosen once. The words come back later.

The second sentence is not decoration; it is the timing contract.

## Proposer / Balancer — Round 2

### Proposer

Use one form card with Words → Choices → Review so the person experiences the
concept without scanning a long intake document.

### Balancer

“Words first” can become pressure to contribute, and the phrase “we each leave”
can make silence feel like refusal. Showing six inputs can still feel like a
quota even if they are labelled optional.

### Reconciliation

- Say “leave a few words, if you want.”
- Say “up to two,” never “six places” in the introduction.
- Give every horizon a visible `optional` label.
- Allow the all-empty path and name it “Continue quietly.”
- Include the line “Quiet belongs here too.”

## Copy Sequence

1. metadata and preview boundary;
2. compact navigation;
3. hero and visual equation;
4. progressive form shell;
5. mechanism disclosure;
6. receipt and footer;
7. error, empty, disabled, and no-script states.

## Validation Questions

- Does the first screen say “time capsule” before “number”?
- Can the reader separate one-time selection from later openings?
- Is the whole-capsule effect clear without fake math?
- Does every contribution phrase preserve voluntary quiet?
- Is there any implied token reward, rank, privacy, or submission?
- Can every line survive outside its visual context without changing meaning?
