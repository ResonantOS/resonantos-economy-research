From one word to one shared number

See how the capsule
holds together.

Each accepted word becomes one place in the capsule. When intake closes, every accepted place is gathered into one manifest and fingerprinted into one root. That root meets a later public value to place the founding supply inside the range we agreed on. The 2-, 5-, and 10-year openings happen after that number is fixed.

Lifecycle

Order is a safety property

The root must freeze before the beacon is observed. The supply must be reproduced before any future release. Moving a step earlier changes what people can influence.

  4. Intake closedAll writes fail.
  5. Manifest frozenAccepted records settle.

Quota: zero to two accepted words per horizon.
Privacy: credential never enters the public manifest.
Finality: releases never alter root or supply.
Parity: public and sealed records have no extra weight.
Consent: no release response means remain sealed.
Authority: participation grants no allocation, rank, reward, or governance.

Dependency boundary

What the capsule does not own

These are not implementation details to fill in quietly. Each is a separate owner gate whose absence blocks the affected live behavior.

Admission

Credential issuance, recovery, revocation, audit, and Sybil posture belong to governance or membership.

Sealed protection

Commitment, encryption, keys, recovery, migration, and no-survivor behavior belong to security, privacy, and custody.

Token space

The real token unit, inclusive band, decimals, overflow, and compatibility belong to token and protocol owners.

Beacon

Provider, round, availability, independence posture, and fallback belong to the mechanism/security decision.

Display safety

Moderation, appeals, harmful reveals, legal change, death, and incapacity belong to content/legal/privacy owners.

Ten-year care

Redundancy, restore, migration, succession, and loss reporting belong to archive and successor owners.

Reader on-ramp

Plain terms for the diagram

These definitions explain the page. They are local reading aids, not promoted canonical definitions.

Word place

One optional position assigned to one future opening. A filled place becomes one record.

Not a fixed amount of tokens.

Envelope

The private six-slot view tied to an opaque credential and used to enforce the quota.

Not a public identity.

Record

The canonical public description of an accepted public word or approved sealed commitment.

Not contributor rank or value.

Capsule root

One authenticated fingerprint of the complete frozen accepted record set.

Not proof of fairness, identity, or consent.

Beacon

A predeclared public value observed only after the root is frozen.

Public does not automatically mean uncontrollable.

Release view

The 2-, 5-, or 10-year display of public and separately confirmed opened words.

Never a supply recalculation.
