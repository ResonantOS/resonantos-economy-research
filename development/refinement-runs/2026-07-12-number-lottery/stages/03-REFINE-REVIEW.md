## Highest-Discrimination Question Preserved

**May the community ritual directly determine total supply, or may it only
produce symbolic/denominational input to a separately justified supply?**

Why it matters: direct determination makes every normalization, participation,
Sybil, privacy, and moderation choice economically load-bearing. The safe
research default is ritual-only or bounded-seed coupling; direct coupling remains
a candidate to attack, not an assumed requirement.

Target decision record: the s05 candidate tournament. Unanswered risk: a design
that feels democratic may turn arbitrary submission mechanics into monetary
policy.

## Dialectic

### Role A — Participatory-Meaning Advocate

- A number with public provenance can become a shared origin myth rather than a
  founder-selected constant.
- Staged reveals can make long-term membership visible and create recurring
  reasons to revisit founding intentions.
- Public and private modes can widen participation if contributors control
  disclosure and can verify cohort inclusion.
- A deterministic derivation can make the process auditable and narratively
  legible.

### Role B — Economy, Privacy, And Authority Skeptic

- A reproducible number can still be arbitrary: the normalization, eligibility,
  ordering, duplicate, and scale rules decide the output.
- Direct supply coupling converts content moderation and Sybil defense into
  financial-policy levers.
- Low-entropy words are guessable from unsalted hashes; “private integer” is not
  a confidentiality guarantee.
- Long horizons create consent drift, death/absence, forgotten-opening,
  platform-failure, and harmful-content risks.
- Participation volume can become symbolic status or implicit authority even if
  formal governance rules say otherwise.

## Reconciliation

| Tension | Decision for research | Residue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Meaning vs arbitrariness | Meaning must come from transparent provenance and ritual, not claims that the number is mathematically special. | community interpretation remains contextual |
| Participation vs economic manipulation | Compare ritual-only, bounded-seed, and direct-supply candidates separately. | external economic owner decides coupling |
| Privacy vs verifiability | Use commitments or disclosed artifacts with an explicit threat model; do not call an integer encrypted. | key/custody and legal review later |
| Permanence vs consent drift | Capsule rules need mode-specific reveal, non-reveal, absence, and stewardship-failure behavior. | revocation after immutable commitment remains a design tension |
| Engagement vs authority capture | Events carry no credit/reward/authority effect by default and require capture checks if that changes. | informal status effects cannot be fully eliminated |
