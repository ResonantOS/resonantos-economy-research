## Direct Answer

Do not derive ResonantDAO's token supply from submitted words, characters,
commitments, ciphertexts, hashes, or their integer representations. The viable
project is a **ritual-only time capsule** that produces a reproducible,
provenance-bearing symbolic integer and commemorative labels while having zero
effect on token supply, rewards, contribution credit, reputation, or governance
authority. Its meaning comes from an admitted cohort, frozen rules, preservation,
and later reflection—not from numerical mysticism or a claim of economic safety.
[Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Candidate mechanisms and §Recommended formal
unit; Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Design options; Kahneman explorer,
`research.md` §Supported claims; `revised-synthesis.md` §Smallest Coherent Unit]

The smallest coherent unit is therefore: an exact public-manifest format,
optional committed-but-unopened artifacts, a frozen ordered cohort root, a
reproducible symbolic integer derived only from the frozen ritual record, and
commemorative labels. Encryption is outside this minimum unit because recovery
adds key, nonce, custody, migration, and disclosure obligations that the current
design has not resolved. [`revised-synthesis.md` §Smallest Coherent Unit; Fritz
explorer, `research.md` §Candidate mechanisms; Quine review,
`reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints]

## Technical Architecture Boundary

### Included in the minimum unit

1. Freeze a versioned submission-artifact schema before intake, including cohort
   identifier, mode, normalization/canonicalization version, duplicate policy,
   close time, leaf ordering, digest identifier, domain tag, and unsigned integer
   byte order. Independent implementations must reproduce the same accepted
   bytes and output. [Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Assumptions and threat
   boundaries; Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack]
2. Admit at most one counted artifact per eligible participant under an explicit
   eligibility rule, while stating that a wallet is not a human and a Merkle leaf
   is not an identity. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation and
   stewardship rules and §Concrete witnesses and failure cases; Quine review,
   `reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints]
3. Canonicalize public structured records using a frozen profile before hashing;
   compute an ordered cohort root and ritual-only integer under named algorithms
   and domain separation. The integer is deterministic but is not thereby random,
   fair, private, or economically calibrated. [RFC 8785](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8785.html);
   [RFC 9162](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc9162.html); Fritz explorer,
   `research.md` §Directly supported claims; Quine review,
   `reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints]
4. Permit public artifacts, committed-but-unopened artifacts, and abstention as
   distinct successful modes. Keep contributor-controlled opening material with
   the contributor unless a later specialist-owned custody design is approved.
   [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation and stewardship rules;
   Kahneman explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses; Popper review,
   `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack]
5. Use the output only for capsule IDs, epoch names, public art, or other
   unambiguously commemorative labels. Every ritual input may change while all
   supply, reward, reputation, credit, and governance fields remain unchanged.
   [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Design options; Popper review,
   `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item 9]

### Explicitly outside the minimum unit

- Encrypted payload recovery is deferred. It requires a named authenticated
  encryption construction, key and nonce generation, associated-data rules,
  custody, succession, deletion/disclosure posture, migration, and a declared
  no-survivor outcome. [NIST SP 800-38D](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/38/d/final);
  Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Candidate mechanisms; Quine review,
  `reviewer-quine.md` §Candidate table]
- Unique-human proof, Sybil resistance, content truth, contributor consent,
  neutral timestamping, economic calibration, and behavioral efficacy are not
  supplied by canonicalization, a digest, a commitment, encryption, or a Merkle
  root. [Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Assumptions and threat boundaries;
  Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and failure cases;
  Kahneman explorer, `research.md` §External-validity limits]
- Token supply selection, token distribution, rewards, governance weight, and
  economic range selection remain outside this project and require their own
  authorized owners and evidence. [`revised-synthesis.md` §Objection
  Disposition; Quine review, `reviewer-quine.md` §Candidate table]

## Artifact and Privacy Distinctions

These terms must not be collapsed:

- A **public artifact** is an accepted versioned record whose content is intended
  for public preservation and later display; public means disclosure is expected
  and durable. [Quine review, `reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints;
  Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation and stewardship rules]
- A **non-public artifact** is not available to the public. This term alone makes
  no claim that the content is encrypted, committed, access-restricted, or
  recoverable. [Quine review, `reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints]
- A **committed-but-unopened artifact** participates through a named commit/open
  construction with stated hiding/binding assumptions; its cohort inclusion does
  not establish the submitter's identity, consent to reveal, truth, or indefinite
  secrecy. [Halevi–Micali 1996](https://shaih.github.io/pubs/hm96.html); Fritz
  explorer, `research.md` §Directly supported claims; Quine review,
  `reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints]
- An **encrypted payload** is ciphertext created by a named encryption scheme
  under explicit key, nonce, and associated-data rules; representing letters or
  bytes as an integer is encoding, not encryption. Recoverability also depends
  on keys and custody surviving. [NIST SP 800-38D](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/38/d/final);
  Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Directly supported claims and §Toy witnesses]
- An **access-restricted record** is controlled by policy or permissions and must
  not be described as confidential merely because access is limited. [Quine
  review, `reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints]

The interface must present neutral public, non-public/committed, and
non-participation paths; explain disclosure consequences before submission; use
a comprehension check; permit a reversible draft; and give “submit nothing”
parity. Disclosure behavior is context-sensitive, so evocative “legacy” language
must not substitute for informed consent. [Kahneman explorer, `research.md`
§Supported claims and §Concrete witnesses and mitigations]

## Nine-Fixture Evidence Plan

These fixtures are specifications for the next evidence boundary. **None has
been implemented or run.** Each must name exact inputs, expected outputs,
failure verdicts, and independent reproducibility criteria before any candidate
can advance beyond fixture-ready research status. [Popper review,
`reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack; `revised-synthesis.md` §Next
Evidence Boundary]

1. **`canonicalization.json`:** include reordered fields, Unicode normalization
   forms, multiple scripts, empty inputs, and malformed records. Expect exact
   accepted records, canonical bytes, digest/root, and identical outputs from two
   independent implementations; any divergence kills the candidate profile.
   [Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item 1]
2. **`cohort-abuse.json`:** include duplicate artifacts/identities and one actor
   controlling 100 wallets. Expect the frozen eligibility/duplicate rule to
   determine exact counted leaves; wallet count must not be reported as unique
   humans. [Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item 2;
   Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and failure cases]
3. **`participation-modes.json`:** include public, committed-but-unopened,
   abstain, and never-open cases. Expect no penalty, rank loss, reward loss, or
   authority change for abstention or non-opening. [Popper review,
   `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item 3]
4. **`harmful-reveal.json`:** include doxxing, harassment, or otherwise prohibited
   content at opening. Expect quarantine and a non-content-bearing disposition
   receipt, no automatic publication, an unchanged cohort root, and no status or
   governance consequence. [Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture
   Pack item 4; Kahneman explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and
   mitigations]
5. **`loss-and-reveal.json`:** include lost opening material/key, early opening
   request, year-2 consent, year-5 refusal, and year-10 silence/death. Expect no
   inferred consent, no forced opening, and a sealed/orphan/no-survivor outcome
   where recovery is unavailable. [Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum
   Fixture Pack item 5; Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and
   failure cases]
6. **`steward-migration.json`:** model steward disappearance and successor
   recovery. Expect the successor to reproduce state/root and publish continuity
   evidence while being unable to change contributor consent, ritual history, or
   supply fields. [Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item
   6; Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and failure cases]
7. **`grinding.json`:** let the final submitter try 10,000 message/nonce variants.
   Expect the test to measure selection advantage; material steering kills the
   intake design and triggers redesign rather than a fairness claim. [Popper
   review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item 7; Fritz explorer,
   `research.md` §Toy witnesses]
8. **`status-capture.json`:** compare founder, whale, steward, technical insider,
   and ordinary participant. Expect no additional inclusion, placement, reveal
   privilege, rank, symbolic weight, reputation, or governance authority for any
   role or donor. [Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item
   8; Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and failure cases]
9. **`economic-boundary.json`:** vary every ritual input/output and every accepted
   participation mode. Expect planned supply to remain exactly
   `1_000_000_000`, with maximum/initial/circulating-supply, reward, credit,
   reputation, and governance fields unchanged. Any mutation is a hard failure.
   This checks separation only; it does not validate 1B economics. [Popper
   review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack item 9;
   `revised-synthesis.md` §Objection Disposition]

## Toy Scenario Expectations

- **Collision:** `"AB"` and `"BA"` must collide under character summation,
  demonstrating why that candidate is killed rather than “improved.” [Fritz
  explorer, `research.md` §Toy witnesses; Popper review,
  `reviewer-popper.md` §Candidate table]
- **Canonical ordering:** logically equivalent objects with reordered fields
  should yield identical canonical bytes under the frozen profile; malformed or
  profile-incompatible records should fail explicitly rather than be silently
  repaired. [Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Toy witnesses; [RFC
  8785](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8785.html)]
- **Modulo bias:** a toy byte mapped by `x mod 10` gives unequal residue counts;
  reject `x >= 250` before modulo in that toy. A real bounded mapping remains
  deferred until an economic owner and exact unbiased algorithm exist. [Fritz
  explorer, `research.md` §Toy witnesses; `revised-synthesis.md` §Objection
  Disposition]
- **Sybil attack:** 100 wallets controlled by one actor must not become 100
  unique humans. The fixture may test an eligibility rule, but it cannot claim a
  universal identity solution. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Concrete
  witnesses and failure cases; Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum
  Fixture Pack]
- **Last-submitter grinding:** a participant able to observe provisional output
  and try variants is expected to gain steering power; commit-before-close and
  delayed collective opening may reduce the attack but do not prove fairness.
  [Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Toy witnesses]
- **Harmful reveal:** content must be quarantined before publication, with a
  disposition receipt that leaves the original cohort root unchanged and grants
  no status benefit or penalty. [Kahneman explorer, `research.md` §Concrete
  witnesses and mitigations; Popper review, `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture
  Pack]
- **Key/opening loss:** an intact ciphertext or root may remain auditable while
  plaintext is permanently unrecoverable; this is an acceptable declared outcome,
  not evidence of successful recovery. [Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Toy
  witnesses; Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and failure
  cases]
- **Privacy-cue effect:** test whether legacy/community framing changes sensitive
  disclosure under otherwise identical risk information. Expected design
  response is warning-first consent and non-participation parity, not a behavioral
  benefit claim. [Kahneman explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and
  mitigations]
- **Narrative/status capture:** founders, donors, stewards, and technically skilled
  contributors must not gain ranking or authority, and flattering selections
  must not erase dissent or exclusions. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md`
  §Concrete witnesses and failure cases; Kahneman explorer, `research.md`
  §Concrete witnesses and mitigations]
- **Memory conflict:** preserve the original artifact beside later interpretation;
  neither overrides the other when confident recollection differs. [Kahneman
  explorer, `research.md` §Supported claims and §Concrete witnesses and
  mitigations]

## Role, Consent, and Stewardship Model

- **Eligible member / submitter / wallet / accepted artifact:** define each
  separately. Admission is a governance rule; one wallet is not one human, and
  one accepted artifact does not create authority. [Quine review,
  `reviewer-quine.md` §Terminology Constraints; Ostrom explorer, `research.md`
  §Concrete witnesses and failure cases]
- **Intake steward:** applies the frozen admission, duplicate, timing, and mode
  rules; cannot modify supply, rank submissions, or change the cohort after
  closure. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation and stewardship rules]
- **Moderation panel:** reviews content against published rules, states reasons,
  supports low-cost appeal, and may quarantine content without rewriting the
  cohort root or changing governance weight. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md`
  §Participation and stewardship rules and §Concrete witnesses and failure
  cases]
- **Archive custodian:** preserves artifacts and integrity material, conducts
  annual drills, and reports loss; custody does not grant opening consent or
  token-policy power. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation and
  stewardship rules]
- **Independent verifier:** reproduces public manifest/root outputs and audits
  continuity without receiving plaintext by default. Verification proves the
  specified computation, not fairness, consent, or unique-human participation.
  [Fritz explorer, `research.md` §Directly supported claims; Popper review,
  `reviewer-popper.md` §Minimum Fixture Pack]
- **Successor council:** is separately governed and can restore continuity after
  steward loss, but cannot change prior consent, silently amend frozen cohorts,
  or acquire supply authority. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation
  and stewardship rules and §Concrete witnesses and failure cases]
- **Contributor consent:** is mode- and epoch-specific. Public submission consent,
  commitment inclusion, opening, and publication are separate acts. Silence,
  incapacity, death, missing key/opening material, or steward failure defaults to
  non-reveal; a predeclared designee may act only where separately found legally
  and ethically valid. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation and
  stewardship rules; Kahneman explorer, `research.md` §Concrete witnesses and
  mitigations]
- **Future-rule participation:** contributors may help revise successor-cohort
  rules, but prior cohorts remain frozen and any correction must create a
  versioned successor. [Ostrom explorer, `research.md` §Participation and
  stewardship rules]
