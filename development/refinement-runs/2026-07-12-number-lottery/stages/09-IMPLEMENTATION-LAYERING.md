# RCBC Implementation Layering

Status: non-executed planning artifact
Target: Ritual Capsule Boundary Contract
Active layer window: L0 only after separate Task Session approval

## Boundary Heuristic

Each layer ends when its next unit adds less evidence for the current decision
than beginning the next decision layer.

`value = decision unlocked + observable outcome + risk reduced`

`cost = implementation + verification + coordination`

## L0 — Specification And Falsifier Contract

After this layer, we know whether RCBC is specified precisely enough that an
independent evidence harness could attempt to falsify it without reopening
product discovery.

- Minimum unit: versioned source/authority contract, common fixture envelope,
  nine complete specification-only fixtures, schema validator, and evidence-run
  criterion.
- Included: exact inputs, expected outputs, failure verdicts, owner handles,
  source snapshot fields, no-effect fields, and non-validation labels.
- Deferred: computation, real artifacts, live members, commitments, encryption,
  policy changes.
- Observable result: machine-readable fixture package validates structurally.
- Risk reduced: vague fixtures and scaffold-as-validation drift.
- Exit evidence: schema validation, fixture completeness report, owner/gap
  matrix, independent review.
- Promotion: only to L1 toy implementation; never to live intake.

## L1 — Deterministic Public Toy Cohort

After this layer, we know whether two independent implementations can reproduce
one toy public manifest/root/integer while the modeled policy state remains
unchanged.

- Minimum unit: synthetic records, canonicalization profile, deterministic
  manifest/root/integer, and PolicyNonEffectCheck.
- Included fixtures: canonicalization, cohort abuse, grinding, economic
  boundary.
- Deferred: real eligibility, live content, events, moderation operations,
  hidden-message paths.
- Observable result: reproducibility and falsification report.
- Risk reduced: ambiguity, collisions, ordering drift, last-submitter steering,
  ritual-to-policy dependency.
- Exit evidence: executable harness receipts and independent reproduction.
- Promotion: only to L2 when L1 has no hard KILL or has a reviewed repair.

## L2 — Stewardship, Consent, And Event Toy Flows

After this layer, we know whether the public baseline can preserve history,
quarantine display, migrate stewardship, and treat non-participation/non-response
without status or policy effects.

- Minimum unit: synthetic disposition overlay and lifecycle state machine.
- Included fixtures: participation modes, harmful reveal, loss/reveal, steward
  migration, status capture.
- Deferred: production moderation, legal compliance claim, real contributor
  data, cryptographic recovery.
- Observable result: transition/adversarial evidence report.
- Risk reduced: consent drift, harmful display, steward loss, founder/whale
  capture.
- Exit evidence: state-transition traces, negative-path receipts, independent
  audit.
- Promotion: only to L3 research; no pilot yet.

## L3 — Committed-Artifact Extension

After this layer, we know whether one named commit/open construction can support
committed-but-unopened artifacts under a documented threat model without
breaking RCBC’s zero-effect and stewardship contracts.

- Minimum unit: frozen security criterion, synthetic commitment fixtures, and
  open/non-open/guessing/grinding cases.
- Included: protocol/parameter selection, contributor-held opening material,
  low-entropy threat, selective opening, algorithm migration.
- Deferred: encrypted recovery, server-held keys, production custody, real
  private content.
- Observable result: security-owner reviewed evidence package.
- Risk reduced: calling hashes commitments or commitments privacy.
- Exit evidence: security review and executable negative fixtures.
- Promotion: L4 remains owner-gated.

## L4 — Pilot/Release Readiness

After this layer, owners could decide whether a bounded pilot is ethically,
legally, socially, operationally, and technically permissible.

- Minimum unit: not currently specifiable.
- Required owners: governance, privacy/legal, security, preservation,
  accessibility/language, behavioral research, token-policy boundary.
- Deferred: encrypted recovery unless separately justified; all economic
  coupling permanently outside RCBC.
- Observable result: readiness decision, possibly `block`.
- Risk reduced: premature public intake and ten-year promise without continuity.
- Exit evidence: owner approvals, evidence harness history, incident/deletion/
  migration plans, public language review.
- Promotion: external owner decision only.

## Non-Regression Guarantees

Every layer preserves:

- no ritual-to-supply/reward/credit/reputation/governance dependency;
- no unique-human claim from wallets/receipts/artifacts;
- no encryption/privacy/fairness/engagement overclaim;
- non-participation and non-response have no penalty;
- historical root and display disposition remain distinct;
- source facts remain versioned and drift-aware;
- fixture specifications remain distinct from executed evidence.

Recommended next layer: L0, only through a separately approved Task Session.
Major deferral: all live intake and hidden-message implementation.
