# Whisper Learning Residue — Number Lottery Presentation

Status: observed composition learning; not canonical author-voice policy

## What Worked

- The approved voice begins with collective choice and felt meaning, then earns
  mechanism language later.
- Short “we choose / we reject / we return” declarations fit this community
  better than procedural reassurance.
- The One/Many contrast gives the trust problem an identity-bearing image.
- “Some words are spoken; some remain sealed” carries the participation boundary
  more naturally than a public/private taxonomy.
- The 2/5/10 sequence works best as a return to a changing community, not as a
  promise of engagement.

## What Failed

- Version one sounded like governance documentation performed aloud.
- “We need to be honest about…” explained the authoring constraint instead of
  speaking to the room.
- Technical trust language before collective desire reduced resonance and made
  the presentation feel defensive.

## Reuse Boundary

These observations apply to this Number Lottery presentation for ResonantDAO
community and founding contributors. Rerun the language audition for investor,
technical, governance-vote, or general-public audiences.
