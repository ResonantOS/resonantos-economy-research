# Number Lottery — Live Presenter Deck

Audience: ResonantDAO community and founding contributors<br>
Room outcome: understand the ritual and want to help shape and participate in it<br>
Estimated duration: 8–10 minutes<br>
Voice: collective, declarative, warm, visionary, and human<br>
Status: complete composition draft; mechanism remains candidate research

## Moment 1 — Opening

### Projected Copy

We could choose any number.

Instead, we choose to make one together.

### Spoken Copy

Forty-two billion. A trillion. A perfectly round number. Any of them could work.
But we make this choice only once. We do not want a number with no memory. We
want the first supply of ResonantDAO to carry something of us.

### Speaker Notes

- Pause after each example number.
- Let “make one together” land before moving on.
- Do not explain the mechanism yet.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: arbitrary choice becomes a founding opportunity.
- Source: operator correction and approved language audition version two.
- Claim boundary: supply is selectable; no number or mechanism is yet approved.

## Moment 2 — From Quantity To Memory

### Projected Copy

A supply is a quantity.

A founding number can become a story.

### Spoken Copy

Most supplies begin as arithmetic. A story is added later. We can reverse that.
We can begin with people, with words, with a ritual—and let the number carry the
memory of how ResonantDAO began.

### Speaker Notes

- Keep this emotional, not economic.
- “Can become” is intentional: meaning is an aspiration, not a measured effect.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: establishes why the community should care.
- Evidence status: normative proposal; no mentality effect claimed.

## Moment 3 — The Word

### Projected Copy

What one word would you send
into ResonantDAO's future?

### Spoken Copy

Do not say it yet. Just hold it for a moment. It could be a hope. A warning. A
promise. A question. Something you want the community of the future to find.

### Speaker Notes

- Give the room 8–10 seconds of silence.
- Do not ask people to reveal their word.
- This is the first audience participation moment.

### Interaction Prompt

Hold one word privately. No answer is requested.

### Authoring Metadata

- Pedagogy: lets the audience experience the idea before hearing the system.
- Question rule: genuine prompt; no answer embedded.

## Moment 4 — More Than One Way To Belong

### Projected Copy

Some words are spoken.

Some remain sealed.

Both belong.

### Spoken Copy

The Many does not ask everyone to appear in the same way. One person may offer
a word for everyone to see. Another may seal a word for the future. Another may
choose silence. The ritual must make room for all three.

### Speaker Notes

- “Sealed” means a future committed-artifact path, not proven encryption.
- Silence and non-participation must not create penalty or lower status.
- Avoid explaining keys, commitments, or custody here.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: moves plurality from slogan into participation modes.
- Source boundary: public, committed, encrypted, and absent remain distinct.

## Moment 5 — Tension

### Projected Copy

One voice can choose a number.

Only the Many can give it meaning.

### Spoken Copy

We reject a number handed down by one founder, one closed room, or one loud
voice. But the Many does not mean giving everything away. Some words can be
shared. Some can remain sealed. Each person enters the ritual on their own
terms.

### Speaker Notes

- Deliver “the Many” as community authorship, not majority domination.
- The mechanism still needs admission and anti-steering evidence.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: makes control and sovereignty visible before mechanism detail.
- Language reference: public Augmentatism One/Many contrast, newly composed.

## Moment 6 — Close The Circle

### Projected Copy

First, the circle closes.

Then, the signal arrives.

### Spoken Copy

The order matters. We gather the words and close the circle before the final
result can be known. Only after that do we bring in a public signal from outside
the closed circle. No one gets to wait for the answer and then rewrite the
beginning.

### Speaker Notes

- This is the first mechanism-facing moment.
- “Public signal” is the human-language placeholder for a future named beacon.
- Do not claim the process is manipulation-proof; say the order is designed to
  reduce last-moment steering.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: answers why closure precedes reveal.
- Evidence status: candidate design; beacon and anti-grinding fixtures unrun.

## Moment 7 — The Shared Seed

### Projected Copy

Words become a shared seed.

The seed becomes the supply.

### Spoken Copy

Before the ritual begins, we choose the space where the number may live. We
choose the rules. We close the circle. Then the words and the final signal form
the shared seed from which the exact initial supply is drawn. The rule is visible
before the answer exists.

### Speaker Notes

- “Space” means the predeclared numerical range or width.
- The mapping procedure, token decimals, overflow behavior, and reproducibility
  are still fixture requirements.
- Do not display formulas in this community introduction.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: explains the selector without losing the ritual.
- Claim boundary: proposed sequence, not validated computation.

## Moment 8 — Reveal

### Projected Copy

Many words.

One founding number.

A future we can return to.

### Spoken Copy

Each of us offers a word. Some are spoken. Some remain sealed. When the circle
closes, the number can finally appear. It becomes our initial supply—but its
story keeps moving. Two years. Five years. Ten years. We return, open another
layer, and meet the future we once imagined.

### Speaker Notes

- Slow down for the three time horizons.
- This is the emotional reveal; avoid adding mechanism detail afterward.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: recomposes number selection and time capsule into one idea.
- Source: approved version-two audition.

## Moment 9 — The Time Arc

### Projected Copy

2 years — Echo

5 years — Divergence

10 years — Constellation

### Spoken Copy

After two years, we place our beginning beside who we have become. After five,
we look for what endured, what changed, and what we chose to leave behind. After
ten, we open another layer of the constellation and hand its care to the next
stewards.

### Speaker Notes

- Mention an annual integrity day verbally only if time allows.
- Reveals remain consent-safe; not every sealed contribution must ever become
  public.
- Names are event concepts, not promises of behavioral impact.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: makes time tangible and memorable.
- Evidence status: event-participation hypothesis.

## Moment 10 — Return, Do Not Worship

### Projected Copy

We return to remember.

We return to disagree.

We return to begin again.

### Spoken Copy

The capsule is not a monument to agreement. It is a place where the Many can see
how it changed. We can translate old words, answer them with new ones, record
what failed, and preserve the voices that refused the dominant story.

### Speaker Notes

- Optional examples: translation circle, newcomer response cohort,
  counter-capsule audit.
- Avoid nostalgia as conformity; dissent is part of the archive.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: protects the capsule from becoming founding mythology.
- Value: plurality through time.

## Moment 11 — The Ethical Floor

### Projected Copy

No word buys influence.

No silence becomes a penalty.

### Spoken Copy

Your word helps shape the founding number. It does not buy rank. It does not
give governance power. Choosing silence does not make anyone less part of the
community. The ritual creates a shared beginning—not a new hierarchy.

### Speaker Notes

- This is a proposed non-collapse boundary, not an implemented guarantee.
- Contribution, reward, reputation, and authority remain separate.

### Interaction Prompt

None.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: states the anti-capture boundary in room-native language.
- Authority effect: none.

## Moment 12 — Invitation

### Projected Copy

Bring one word.

Help shape the ritual.

Build the founding number with us.

### Spoken Copy

Today, we are not asking you to submit the word. We are asking you to help build
the ritual worthy of receiving it. Help us choose the boundaries. Help us decide
what can be shared, what may remain sealed, how the circle closes, and what we
want to meet again in two, five, and ten years. Then, when the ritual is ready,
we begin together.

### Speaker Notes

- End with a concrete next step chosen by the organizer: design circle, working
  group, or expression-of-interest form.
- Do not collect real private words at this presentation.
- Leave the final line on screen during discussion.

### Interaction Prompt

Invite people to join the design circle; do not solicit capsule content.

### Authoring Metadata

- Story state: converts inspiration into bounded co-design participation.
- CTA status: safe pre-launch invitation.
